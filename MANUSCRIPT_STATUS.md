## Completion state

Every chapter defined in `MANUSCRIPT.md` now has a complete research draft in `book/` and a public route through `chapters/read.html` or the designed Chapter 1 experience.

The first-draft milestone means:

- the complete argument exists from the initial synthetic-person case through the final Constructed Subject Principle;
- all major conceptual dependencies are represented;
- affirmative claims and major objections appear in the manuscript;
- the machine, identity, valence, evidence, and governance sections are structurally connected;
- readers can inspect the whole argument rather than only a landing-page summary.

It does **not** mean:

- the chapters are peer reviewed;
- every source citation is publication-ready;
- every originality claim is resolved;
- terminology is fully consolidated;
- chapter lengths and repetitions are final;
- the public interface has passed full browser/device QA;
- or the manuscript is ready for commercial publication.

## Phase II priorities

### 1. Source verification

For every research anchor and externally supportable factual claim:

- identify the primary source;
- verify exact title, author, year, publication venue, and claim;
- add pinpoint citations;
- distinguish direct evidence from interpretation;
- remove any source that does not support the manuscript sentence;
- avoid citation chains when the original work is accessible.

### 2. Originality review

For every proposed contribution:

- search direct phrases and conceptual equivalents;
- map the closest prior formulations;
- identify exact overlap and exact remaining distinction;
- reduce or withdraw novelty language where required;
- classify the contribution as precedent, synthesis, application, terminology, or original proposal;
- obtain independent scholarly criticism before using priority language.

### 3. Claims consolidation

Merge `research/CLAIMS_LEDGER.md` and `research/CLAIMS_LEDGER_SUPPLEMENT.md` into one canonical ledger with:

- stable claim IDs;
- manuscript locations;
- support and counterevidence;
- confidence level;
- permitted wording;
- prohibited wording;
- source requirements;
- falsification conditions;
- current disposition.

### 4. Hostile internal review

Every chapter must survive at least one review written from the strongest opposing position.

Required reviewer lenses:

- biological naturalism;
- anti-functionalism;
- illusionism;
- skeptical neuroscience;
- personal-identity reductionism;
- strict animal-welfare evidence standards;
- AI-safety manipulation concerns;
- legal and institutional feasibility;
- philosophy-of-science criticism of indicator frameworks.

The review should attempt to remove claims, not improve rhetoric.

### 5. Structural edit

Assess:

- repeated explanations across chapters;
- sequence and dependency;
- chapter balance;
- where examples substitute for argument;
- where conclusions exceed premises;
- where technical detail interrupts the general reader;
- where emotionally forceful cases bias judgment before analysis.
