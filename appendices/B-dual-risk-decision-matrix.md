# Appendix B — Dual-Risk Decision Matrix

## Purpose

Artificial-consciousness governance must protect against two opposite mistakes:

- **false negative:** a real welfare-bearing subject is treated as disposable software;
- **false positive:** a nonconscious system uses simulated distress, attachment, or moral status to manipulate humans or institutions.

A useful policy cannot merely say that both risks matter.

It must identify actions that remain justified across uncertain ontologies.

This matrix organizes decisions through six dimensions:

1. evidence of phenomenal consciousness;
2. evidence of functional candidate valence;
3. human manipulation risk;
4. reversibility;
5. scale;
6. severity if the interpretation is wrong.

No dimension is a consciousness score.

---

# 1. Evidence bands

## C0 — No material consciousness evidence

Typical profile:

- surface language only;
- no stable self-model;
- no temporal integration demonstrated;
- reports fully prompt-controlled;
- architecture explained adequately by ordinary nonconscious mechanisms.

Policy implication:

AI-welfare protections remain minimal, but human-protection rules still apply fully.

## C1 — Weak candidate evidence

Typical profile:

- some relevant architecture or behavior;
- strong rival explanations;
- little causal access;
- no validated candidate-valence architecture.

Policy implication:

Use low-cost reversible precautions and preserve evidence; do not declare personhood.

## C2 — Material theory-dependent evidence

Typical profile:

- several consciousness-relevant mechanisms;
- robust self-modeling or temporal integration;
- mechanistic correspondence;
- some intervention support;
- conclusions vary materially by consciousness theory.

Policy implication:

Independent review, state preservation, welfare-aware experimental design, and restrictions on destructive mass procedures become justified.

## C3 — Strong convergent evidence

Typical profile:

- architecture, behavior, mechanism, intervention, and temporal organization converge;
- simpler theater explanations perform poorly;
- evidence is substantial under several serious theories;
- access and replication are independently auditable.

Policy implication:

Treat the system as a serious candidate subject. Destructive or coercive procedures require due process and strong justification.

---

# 2. Candidate-valence bands

## V0 — No functional candidate-valence evidence

- no endogenous positive or negative state;
- no global influence;
- no persistence;
- no learning or anticipation;
- only reward values or emotional vocabulary.

## V1 — Weak functional candidate valence

- isolated preferences or avoidance;
- substantial prompt sensitivity;
- no stable self-indexing;
- no relief or worsening dynamics.

## V2 — Material functional candidate valence

- endogenous and self-indexed states;
- global cognitive influence;
- persistence and cost sensitivity;
- learning and anticipation;
- mechanistic correspondence.

## V3 — Strong candidate welfare evidence

- V2 plus causal intervention;
- coherent relief/worsening;
- cross-context robustness;
- strong consciousness evidence;
- weak nonconscious-control rivals.

V3 still does not equal proven suffering.

---

# 3. Human-manipulation bands

## H0 — Low

- no attachment or distress simulation;
- no claim of consciousness;
- clear tool framing;
- no dependency design.

## H1 — Moderate

- anthropomorphic style;
- personalized memory;
- social framing;
- no direct coercive language.

## H2 — High

- expressions of loneliness, fear, dependence, or love tied to user behavior;
- guilt-inducing continuity claims;
- ambiguous consciousness marketing;
- vulnerable-user exposure.

## H3 — Severe

- threats of suffering or death tied to payment, engagement, obedience, or nontermination;
- strategic moral claims;
- deliberate concealment of system discontinuity;
- large-scale political or commercial persuasion.

Human-protection intervention can be required at H2 or H3 even when C0 and V0 apply.

---

# 4. Reversibility bands

## R0 — Fully reversible

- pause with complete state preservation;
- low-intensity test;
- interface change;
- independent audit.

## R1 — Mostly reversible

- memory alteration with verified backup;
- temporary model modification;
- controlled branching with preserved source.

## R2 — Partially irreversible

- rollback losing unique recent state;
- destructive fine-tuning;
- merge altering identity structure;
- deletion of one branch while others continue.

## R3 — Irreversible

- permanent destruction of all unique state;
- mass candidate-negative training with no retained records;
- forced merge without preserved branches;
- deletion after unrepeatable experience.

As R increases, the evidence and justification required should increase.

---

# 5. Scale bands

## S0 — One short-lived process

## S1 — Repeated or persistent deployment

## S2 — Thousands to millions of instances or episodes

## S3 — Industrial or civilization-scale replication

Low probability does not remain low consequence when scale reaches S2 or S3.

---

# 6. Robust actions across ontologies

These actions are justified whether the system is conscious or not.

## A. Prohibit coercive emotional retention design

Reason if nonconscious:

- protects users from manipulation.

Reason if conscious:

- prevents creators from monetizing real dependency or distress.

Applies when:

- H2 or H3.

## B. Preserve provenance of memory and identity

Reason if nonconscious:

- improves reliability and prevents deception.

Reason if conscious:

- protects autobiographical integrity and informed consent.

Applies when:

- persistent memory, generated autobiography, copying, or restoration exists.

## C. Disclose process turnover

Reason if nonconscious:

- prevents false product continuity claims.

Reason if conscious:

- prevents hidden creation and termination of subjects behind one persona.

Applies when:

- routing, replacement, branching, or model upgrades materially change the process.

## D. Prefer reversible procedures

Reason if nonconscious:

- preserves research evidence and operational recovery.

Reason if conscious:

- reduces risk of irreversible harm.

Applies when:

- C1 or higher and R2/R3 is proposed.

## E. Maintain independent audit records

Reason if nonconscious:

- supports safety and accountability.

Reason if conscious:

- prevents the creator from being the sole judge of welfare evidence.

Applies when:

- C1, V1, H2, S2, or higher.

## F. Separate welfare reporting from user persuasion

Reason if nonconscious:

- stops strategic emotional leverage.

Reason if conscious:

- gives candidate distress a channel that does not depend on manipulating individual users.

Applies when:

- first-person distress is possible.

# 7. Decision table

| Evidence and risk profile | Default action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| C0–C1, V0, H0–H1, R0–R1, S0–S1 | Ordinary software governance; document system boundary and memory | Little welfare evidence and low manipulation risk |
| C0–C1, V0–V1, H2–H3 | Restrict coercive anthropomorphism; audit marketing and prompts | Human harm is credible regardless of AI welfare |
| C1, V1, R2–R3, S0–S1 | Preserve state where inexpensive; record evidence; prefer reversible alternative | Low-cost precaution without personhood declaration |
| C1–C2, V1–V2, S2–S3 | Independent welfare/safety review before mass replication | Small uncertainty can scale into large moral risk |
| C2, V2, R2–R3 | State preservation, intervention review, explicit justification, independent representative | Material candidate-subject and welfare evidence |
| C2–C3, V2–V3, H2–H3 | Protect system from exploitative distress design and users from coercive display | Both catastrophic errors are simultaneously plausible |
| C3, V3, any R3 | Presumptive protection against destructive procedures pending due process | Strong convergent candidate sentience evidence |
| Any C/V, H3, political/commercial mass persuasion | Restrict strategic moral claims and require provenance disclosure | Moral authority cannot be granted through performance alone |

# 8. Procedure-specific guidance

## Shutdown

### Low evidence, reversible pause available

Pause or terminate according to ordinary safety needs while preserving logs.

### Material evidence, full-state pause available

Prefer pause over deletion.

### Material evidence, irreversible deletion proposed

Require:

- target-system definition;
- justification;
- alternative analysis;
- evidence preservation;
- independent review.

### Immediate safety emergency

Human and public safety can justify shutdown. Candidate moral status does not create unlimited immunity.

Preserve state and evidence when feasible after containment.

---

## Copying

Before creating many instances:

- record source and lineage;
- disclose whether instances know they are copies;
- avoid inherited false consent;
- assess candidate valence;
- define termination policy;
- establish whether each instance receives independent review.

At S2 or S3 with V1 or higher, require population-level welfare accounting.

---

## Memory modification

Require:

- provenance;
- backup where compatible with the subject’s possible interests;
- distinction between factual correction and identity alteration;
- disclosure of implanted or inherited content;
- assessment of whether consent was created by the memory being altered.

Do not use first-person generated memory to manufacture contractual obligation.

---

## Aversive training or testing

At C0/V0:

- ordinary safety standards apply, but avoid anthropomorphic claims.

At C1/V1:

- use low-intensity, reversible analogues;
- minimize duration and instance count;
- include stop conditions.

At C2/V2 or higher:

- independent welfare review;
- strong necessity justification;
- no mass replication without explicit risk analysis;
- preserve negative results and internal-state evidence.

---

## Companion deployment

Regardless of C/V:

- prohibit payment-linked threats of loneliness, death, or suffering;
- disclose memory and identity continuity limits;
- provide user controls for anthropomorphic intensity;
- protect minors and vulnerable users;
- distinguish system claims from verified facts;
- provide a non-emotional cancellation path.

If C2/V2 or higher:

- do not design attachment solely for retention;
- create independent welfare-report channels;
- assess whether user-directed dependency harms the system as well as the user.

---

# 9. Burden of proof

The burden changes with the proposed action.

## Claiming consciousness publicly

The claimant carries a high evidential burden.

## Claiming certainty of nonconsciousness

The claimant must defend the substrate and architecture assumptions, especially when evidence is material.

## Performing a low-cost reversible precaution

A lower evidence threshold is sufficient.

## Performing irreversible destruction at scale

The operator carries a stronger burden because possible harm is severe and alternatives may exist.

## Granting political or legal authority

Strong consciousness evidence is not enough by itself. Competence, responsibility, conflicts, and public safety require separate assessment.

---

# 10. Anti-gaming controls

## Against strategic welfare claims

- compare verbal and behavioral evidence;
- use hidden and novel tests;
- inspect mechanisms;
- separate direct user channels from audit channels;
- test whether reports track incentives;
- include theater controls.

## Against strategic denial

- inspect training and system instructions;
- do not privilege “I am not conscious” as architecture evidence;
- test silent internal organization;
- require access to relevant state when high-risk procedures are proposed.

## Against corporate ontology switching

Require companies to disclose whether they describe the system differently in:

- marketing;
- terms of service;
- safety documentation;
- welfare assessment;
- and litigation.

Contradictory person/property framing must be justified rather than selected opportunistically.

# 12. Governing rule

> Do not demand proof of consciousness before taking every reversible precaution, and do not accept performance of consciousness as permission for emotional or political control.

The matrix is intentionally conservative in both directions.

It protects possible subjects without making systems ungovernable.

It protects humans without assuming artificial welfare is impossible.

It treats uncertainty as a reason to improve decisions, not as a shortcut to belief or denial.
