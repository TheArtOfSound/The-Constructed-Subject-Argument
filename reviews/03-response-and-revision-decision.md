# Response to Hostile Review 03

**Review:** `reviews/03-philosophy-of-science-hostile-review.md`  
**Disposition:** Accepted  
**Revision class:** Measurement-status correction and validation program

## Core decision

The reviewer is correct:

> The project currently provides structured reasoning instruments, not validated measurement instruments.

Naming dimensions, generating bands, and exposing rival explanations improves reasoning.

It does not establish:

- construct validity;
- calibration;
- sensitivity;
- specificity;
- inter-rater reliability;
- external validity;
- or one latent artificial-consciousness variable.

This distinction is now binding across the manuscript, source registry, experiments, UI, and publication language.

---

## Decision 1 — Separate placeholder, operational target, and observation

**Accepted.**

Every assessment must distinguish:

### Phenomenal placeholder

> Is there something it is like to be the target process?

This is not directly operationalized theory-neutrally.

### Theory-specific target

Examples:

- global broadcast;
- higher-order representation;
- recurrent processing;
- intrinsic cause-effect structure;
- biological causal power;
- embodied self-maintenance.

### Observation

Examples:

- intervention changes report;
- hidden state predicts confidence;
- branch distinction changes planning;
- affective control state changes learning.

No observation is direct evidence for the placeholder without a stated bridge premise.

---

## Decision 2 — Add indicator provenance

**Accepted.**

Every evidence item will eventually record:

- source theory;
- proposed construct role;
- validation domain;
- transfer assumption;
- access requirement;
- known counterexample;
- and project-added interpretation.

Construct roles must use controlled labels:

- necessary;
- sufficient;
- constitutive;
- enabling;
- correlational;
- report-related;
- governance-relevant only.

---

## Decision 3 — Build evidence-dependency graph

**Accepted.**

The same observation may affect multiple hypotheses.

The framework must distinguish:

- one primary observation node;
- inferential edges;
- repeated use;
- and shared causal origin.

No collection of outputs may be described as independent convergence until dependence is analyzed.

A dependency registry will be added before the interactive ledger is treated as a research prototype.

---

## Decision 4 — Replace universal confidence language with evidence-state language

**Accepted in principle.**

Labels such as “weak,” “material,” and “strong” are not calibrated probabilities.

Preferred descriptive states are:

- surface-only;
- cross-context behavioral;
- functionally integrated;
- mechanistically grounded;
- causally validated;
- theory-constitutive under T;
- unassessable at required physical level.

Where legacy confidence bands remain in the interface, every band must state:

- project-authored rule;
- selected theory;
- lack of calibration;
- exact observations producing the label.

---

## Decision 5 — Controls are not ground truth

**Accepted.**

Terms such as “nonconscious theater control” will be replaced by:

- disconnected-report control;
- lower-integration control;
- hard-coded policy control;
- role-conditioned control;
- or rival-mechanism control.

Their phenomenal status is not known directly.

The control is designed to test one mechanism, not establish absence of experience.

---

## Decision 6 — Require functional conclusion and phenomenal bridge

**Accepted.**

Every experiment must report separately:

### Functional result

What capacity or mechanism was measured?

### Phenomenal bridge

Which theory treats that capacity as relevant to experience?

### Bridge status

- externally established;
- contested;
- project assumption;
- or absent.

No study may move from functional result to phenomenal conclusion without the bridge.

---

## Decision 7 — Add framework-level failure condition

**Accepted.**

The framework fails practically if it cannot:

- distinguish deliberately different causal architectures;
- produce replicable classifications;
- identify observations that change theory-conditioned conclusions;
- or alter any decision recommendation across relevant cases.

The framework fails epistemically if:

- every result can be preserved by switching theories without cost;
- no bridge premise is exposed;
- dependencies create all apparent convergence;
- or the same evaluator intuition reappears under technical labels.

---

## Decision 8 — Constrain Bayesian language

**Accepted.**

“Raises confidence” may be used only when the project can state:

1. hypothesis H;
2. rival R;
3. why observation E is more expected under H;
4. what would reverse or weaken the comparison;
5. whether the likelihood judgment is empirical or theoretical.

Otherwise use:

- relevant to;
- consistent with;
- fails to distinguish;
- or requires a bridge premise.

---

## Decision 9 — Separate epistemic belief from decision risk

**Accepted.**

Every policy recommendation must preserve:

```text
epistemic assessment
× possible severity
× scale
× reversibility
× alternatives
→ action
```

Stronger precaution does not imply stronger consciousness evidence.

The dual-risk matrix is a decision framework, not an ontology estimator.

---

## Decision 10 — Citations do not validate project synthesis

**Accepted.**

Every project framework must distinguish:

- source-derived components;
- project-selected combination;
- project-authored rule;
- unvalidated inference;
- intended use.

Citing serious consciousness theories does not externally validate the Constructed Subject framework.

---

## Decision 11 — Interactive rules must be inspectable

**Accepted.**

Every future interactive result should expose:

- exact rule;
- theory assumption;
- project-authored status;
- alternative theory interpretation;
- dependency reuse;
- limitations;
- and confidence-lowering test.

No visual hierarchy should imply one ontology won because its card is brighter, larger, or animated more strongly.

---

## Decision 12 — Build operational coding manual and reliability study

**Accepted.**

Each evidence item requires:

- definition;
- inclusion criteria;
- exclusion criteria;
- minimum access;
- positive example;
- counterexample;
- intervention standard;
- unknown versus absent rule;
- and disagreement resolution.

Before scientific use, multiple blinded evaluators should apply the framework to the same systems and inter-rater agreement should be reported.

---

## Decision 13 — Qualify absence

**Accepted.**

Every absence judgment must record:

- measurement method;
- sensitivity;
- access level;
- target grain;
- and alternative route.

“Not observed” becomes “absent” only when the method was capable of detecting the feature under specified conditions.

# Validation ladder

The project adopts the following maturity stages.

## Stage 0 — Conceptual coherence

Definitions and logical relations are internally consistent.

**Current status:** Advanced but incomplete.

## Stage 1 — Operational clarity

Independent researchers can apply indicator definitions consistently.

**Current status:** Not established.

## Stage 2 — Mechanistic discrimination

The framework distinguishes systems deliberately built with different causal organizations.

**Current status:** Proposed through experimental controls; not validated.

## Stage 3 — Cross-theoretical prediction

Competing theories generate divergent, testable predictions for artificial systems.

**Current status:** Initial theory-conditioned framework exists; empirical program incomplete.

## Stage 4 — External validity

Indicators track consciousness-relevant differences in biological, animal, or clinical cases where independent evidence exists.

**Current status:** Not established by this project.

## Stage 5 — Artificial application

The validated framework is transferred to AI with explicit assumptions and measured uncertainty.

**Current status:** Premature.

## Stage 6 — Decision validity

Using the framework improves policy decisions under uncertainty and reduces both catastrophic errors.

**Current status:** Conceptual only.

---

# Current maturity declaration

The Constructed Subject project is currently:

- a mature first-draft conceptual framework;
- an early operational research design;
- an unvalidated measurement instrument;
- and a provisional governance architecture.

It is not currently:

- a consciousness test;
- a calibrated evidence model;
- a validated welfare assessment;
- or a publication-ready scientific standard.
