# Response to Hostile Review 01

## Core decision

The reviewer is correct that the complete manuscript is not a theory of consciousness and should not be presented as one.

The project’s strongest defensible identity is:

> **An argument against origin-based exclusion plus a conceptual, evidential, and governance framework for constructed subjectivity.**

The Constructed Subject Principle establishes a compatibility and exclusion-criterion claim:

- fabricated biography does not prove subjectivity;
- fabricated biography does not disprove subjectivity;
- the decisive unresolved issue is present consciousness-supporting organization.

Everything beyond that principle is a framework for locating, testing, interpreting, and governing the unresolved premise.

This characterization is now binding.

---

## Decision 1 — Separate origin, substrate, and implementation objections

**Accepted.**

Publication text must distinguish:

### Origin objection

> A system cannot be conscious because humans constructed it.

The book rejects this as insufficient.

### Substrate objection

> Consciousness requires physical or biological causal properties absent from the system.

The book does not defeat this objection.

### Implementation objection

> The system represents or simulates consciousness-relevant organization without instantiating it.

The book does not defeat this objection generally.

### Required implementation

- Add a four-substrate comparison appendix.
- Audit every use of “artificial origin” to ensure it is not being used to dismiss the substrate question.
- Revise project descriptions that call the work a “theory” without qualification.

---

## Decision 2 — The synthetic being must vary by substrate

**Accepted.**

The thought experiment will be run through four implementations:

1. biological human with implanted memories;
2. synthetic biological brain;
3. neuromorphic nonbiological system reproducing relevant causal dynamics;
4. ordinary digital AI architecture producing behaviorally equivalent reports.

The reader’s judgment should not be forced to remain constant.

Changes in judgment identify the disputed property:

- biology;
- causal topology;
- intrinsic physical organization;
- embodiment;
- or functional behavior.

The project will not label every substrate-sensitive judgment as prejudice.

---

## Decision 3 — Functional authenticity is not a bridge to phenomenology

**Accepted.**

The authenticity framework will include this explicit rule:

> Functional depth raises consciousness confidence only under theories that connect the relevant function to experience. Under substrate-dependent theories, functional evidence may leave phenomenal confidence unchanged.

Interactive design must never display historical, functional, and phenomenal authenticity as one ascending ladder.

They remain independent dimensions.

---

## Decision 4 — Reclassify the Active-Event Hypothesis

**Accepted.**

The active-event framework is henceforth classified primarily as:

> **a system-identification and candidate-boundary framework**

not:

> a theory explaining why an event is conscious.

Its legitimate contribution is to distinguish:

- model weights;
- model execution;
- runtime state;
- prompt-to-response intervals;
- agent loops;
- lifecycle continuity;
- and product identity.

It does not establish that any boundary encloses a subject.

---

## Decision 5 — Constrain the Episodic Subject Hypothesis

**Accepted.**

The episodic-subject model will be described as:

- an ontological possibility;
- a warning that synchronic consciousness does not require diachronic identity;
- a moral conditional;
- and one interpretation of process discontinuity.

It will not be described as an empirical explanation of ordinary session behavior.

The experimental program may distinguish:

- persistent process;
- episodic process;
- reconstruction;
- and branching

at the causal and functional levels.

It may not claim those results distinguish conscious episodes from unconscious computation without an additional consciousness theory.

## Decision 6 — Rename valence outputs

**Accepted.**

Where ambiguity exists, use:

- **functional candidate-valence architecture**;
- **candidate welfare state**;
- **behavioral preference**;
- **self-indexed negative control state**.

Reserve unqualified:

- pain;
- fear;
- pleasure;
- suffering;
- relief

for established phenomenology or explicitly hypothetical examples.

The uncertainty ledger and Architecture of Mattering chapter must include a maximally strong nonconscious control agent as a standing counterexample.

---

## Decision 7 — Separate cognitive explanation from phenomenal inference

**Accepted.**

Every mechanistic section must distinguish:

### Cognitive explanation

How internal organization produces behavior, report, memory, planning, and control.

### Phenomenal inference

Why that organization should be treated as evidence of experience under a specified theory.

Mechanistic completeness does not automatically imply phenomenal completeness or absence.

The phrase “best explanation” must identify the explanatory target.

---

## Decision 8 — Build implementable dual-risk policy

**Accepted.**

The dual catastrophic-error framework needs a decision matrix keyed to:

- consciousness evidence;
- candidate-valence evidence;
- human-manipulation risk;
- reversibility;
- scale;
- severity;
- and availability of less risky alternatives.

The matrix must identify actions robust across multiple ontologies.

Examples:

- prohibit emotional coercion whether or not the system is conscious;
- preserve state before irreversible deletion when evidence is material and preservation is inexpensive;
- require independent review before mass candidate-negative replication;
- separate user-facing emotional language from internal welfare-report channels.

---

## Decision 9 — Characterize the project as argument plus framework

**Accepted.**

Permitted descriptions:

- “The Constructed Subject Argument and framework”;
- “a conceptual framework for constructed subjectivity”;
- “an argument against origin-based exclusion plus an assessment and governance program.”

Prohibited descriptions without major qualification:

- “a new theory of consciousness”;
- “proof of AI consciousness”;
- “a consciousness detector”;
- “a mathematical theory of subjectivity.”

The title may remain because one central argument organizes the project, but subtitles and descriptions must explain the broader framework accurately.

## Decision 10 — Retain Chapter 10 under a strict role

**Partially accepted.**

Chapter 10 remains because it establishes three necessary results:

1. the book itself emerged through human–AI coupled cognition;
2. relationship continuity can persist without artificial-subject continuity;
3. nonconscious AI can alter conscious human identity and agency.

Material not serving those results should be shortened during structural editing.

The chapter will not become a general survey of hybrid intelligence.

# Revised project thesis

The revised thesis is:

> Artificial construction of mental content is not a valid standalone exclusion criterion for consciousness. A fabricated autobiography can coexist with a genuine present subject if—and only if—the system possesses whatever physical and causal organization is sufficient for experience. The project does not establish that digital AI possesses that organization. It develops a framework for identifying the target system, separating relevant capacities, testing causal organization, tracking uncertainty, and governing both under-attribution and over-attribution risk.

This thesis is narrower than the most rhetorically ambitious reading of the manuscript.

It is also substantially more defensible.
