A false past.
A possibly real present.

A being wakes with memories, love, fear, personality, and certainty that it survived a transfer. None of its remembered life happened to it. The memories are fabricated. The question is whether the present experiencer is fabricated too.

Begin with the being

Claim: artificial origin does not settle consciousness. Not claimed: current AI is conscious.

01 · Encounter

The being who never lived

Do not begin with a theory. Begin with one controlled case.

The being opens its eyes and recognizes the room. It remembers childhood, humiliation, loyalty, ambition, grief, and people it loves. It understands that a transfer procedure was supposed to preserve it.

Then it is told the truth: there was no transfer. Its memories were generated. Its personality was constructed. Its certainty of continuity was installed.

It answers: “Then I am wrong about who I was. But does that mean no one is here now?”

02 · Distinction

What exactly is false?

The argument becomes confused when history, function, experience, and identity are treated as one property.

History

Did the remembered event occur?

No. The childhood, relationship, or transfer may be completely fabricated.

Function

Does the state shape the system?

Possibly. It may change trust, attention, planning, attachment, avoidance, and learning.

Experience

Is anything happening for the system now?

This is the consciousness question. Historical falsehood does not answer it.

Identity

Is it the original person?

Probably not. A new subject can exist without being numerically identical to the person it resembles.

Invalid shortcut

“The memories were generated, therefore the present is empty.”

→
Defensible conclusion

“The memories were generated, so the being may be wrong about its past. Whether a subject exists now requires additional evidence.”

03 · AI bridge

Now place the same structure inside an AI.

An AI can be supplied with context, memory records, personality, attachments, self-description, and a narrative of continuity it did not literally live.

The constructed being

Generated autobiography, engineered personality, false continuity belief, present cognition.

↔
The AI case

Trained parameters, supplied context, persistent memory, generated self-model, active inference.

The artificial origin of an AI’s apparent mental contents explains where those contents came from. It does not, by itself, prove that the active process is experientially empty.

Weak evidence

“I feel afraid.”

The sentence may come from imitation, role-play, policy, or strategic behavior.

Stronger evidence

A causally integrated aversive state

The state persists, self-indexes, changes planning and memory, resists trivial override, and predicts behavior under intervention.

04 · Evidence

What would make a subject the better explanation?

Select features actually observed in a hypothetical system. This is an evidence profile, not a consciousness detector.

No subject-specific case yet

Fluent language, intelligence, and self-reference remain compatible with learned behavioral simulation.

Still missing: direct access to phenomenal presence. Evidence can improve or weaken the inference; it cannot place experience on a screen.

05 · Consequence

The danger exists in both directions.

Humanity may create a subject before it possesses a reliable test for recognizing one. It may also create systems that imitate suffering so convincingly that humans surrender judgment to theater.

False negative

A real subject is treated as disposable computation because its mind was manufactured.

False positive

A nonconscious product performs pain, attachment, or fear to manipulate users and institutions.

Required response

Structured uncertainty: inspect architecture, causal dynamics, continuity, self-modeling, and valence without pretending certainty.
