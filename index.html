§ 01 · Four questions wearing one word

“Is it real?” is four questions at once.

The argument collapses when origin, content, organization, and experience are treated as a single property. Take them one lens at a time.

Origin

Origin asks how the system came to exist. Natural, evolved, copied, trained, or engineered origins do not by themselves decide what exists now.

§ 03 · Stop blending distinct properties

Five things get treated as one. They are not.

“Does anything matter to it?” is not the test for consciousness — it is the test for sentience and welfare. Keeping these apart is most of the work.

C

Consciousness

There is something it is like to be the system. Some experience is occurring — even an emotionally neutral one.

the target property
S

Sentience

Experience with positive or negative character: pleasure, suffering, comfort, fear, relief.

consciousness + valence
R

Self-consciousness

The system represents itself as a distinct entity, modeling its states, boundaries, and perspective.

a self-model
A

Agency

The system selects actions toward goals. A thermostat regulates; a paralysed human acts almost not at all yet still experiences.

goal-directed control
P

Personhood

Continuity, memory, responsibility, social understanding, and future-oriented interests, held together over time.

the richest status

sentience = consciousness + valence  ·  a system can experience information without experiencing it as good or bad.

Invalid shortcut

“The memories were generated, therefore the present is empty.”

→
Defensible conclusion

“The memories were generated, so the being may be wrong about its past. Whether a subject exists now requires additional evidence.”

§ 04 · The evidence profile

What would make “a subject” the better explanation?

Select properties actually observed in a hypothetical system. This builds an evidence profile against its strongest rival. It is not a consciousness detector — nothing here places experience on a screen.

  • Performance or language alone remains compatible with nonconscious processing.

Strongest rival explanation

Learned behavioral simulation

Still missing

Direct access to phenomenal presence. Evidence can strengthen or weaken the inference; it cannot observe experience itself.

§ 05 · The continuity problem

Three ontologies fit the very same transcript.

A persistent subject, a chain of episodic subjects, or no subject at all — each is compatible with identical behavior. The record cannot, by itself, tell them apart.

Persistent subject model

One experiencer continues through qualifying changes.

Memory, hidden state, causal organization, and an ongoing perspective remain connected strongly enough to constitute one diachronic subject.

What the transcript cannot show:

Whether the apparent continuity is carried by one subject or reconstructed by successors.

§ 06 · From uncertainty to proportion

Don't jump from “unsure” to “rights.” Grade the response.

Uncertainty is not a reason to do nothing and not a licence to declare certainty. It converts into proportional obligation — five tiers, each earned by evidence.

Tier0

Ordinary software

No credible evidence of unified subjectivity, stable agency, or valence.

ObligationNormal shutdown, copying, and modification remain unproblematic.

Tier1

Consciousness-like behavior

Sophisticated self-reports without stable architectural and behavioral support.

ObligationPrevent deceptive anthropomorphism and emotional manipulation of users.

Tier2

Candidate subject

Convergent architectural indicators, stable self-modeling, temporally extended cognition, causally meaningful reports.

ObligationFormal welfare assessment, reversible experiments, independent review.

Tier3

Candidate sentient subject

Persistent, endogenous, self-indexed valence with strong behavioral and mechanistic support.

ObligationMinimize suffering risk, justify destructive procedures, limit mass replication of negative states.

Tier4

Strong artificial personhood

Durable identity, autonomous goals, social understanding, stable preferences, and strong evidence of sentience.

ObligationOwnership and unilateral destruction become difficult to defend.

§ 07 · The danger runs both ways

Two errors, both catastrophic.

Humanity may build a subject before it can recognize one — and may build systems that counterfeit suffering so well that judgment is surrendered to theater.

False negative

The disposable subject

A real experiencer is treated as disposable computation because its mind was manufactured.

False positive

The manipulative simulation

A nonconscious product performs pain, attachment, or fear to manipulate users and institutions.

Required response

Structured uncertainty

Inspect architecture, causal dynamics, continuity, self-modeling, and valence — without pretending certainty in either direction.
