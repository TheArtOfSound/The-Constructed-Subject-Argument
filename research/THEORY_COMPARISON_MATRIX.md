# Consciousness Theory Comparison Matrix

**Purpose:** Control how Chapter 6 and later artificial-subjectivity chapters use scientific theories of consciousness.

**Status:** Research control document, not a consensus statement and not a consciousness detector.

## Governing rule

A theory name may not be used as a decorative endorsement. Any claim that an artificial system is "workspace-like," "recurrent," "integrated," "predictive," "metacognitive," "embodied," or "self-modeling" must specify:

1. the exact theory family being invoked;
2. the level of the claim: correlate, enabling condition, mechanism, or constitutive condition;
3. the relevant substrate commitment;
4. the measurable system property;
5. the strongest nonconscious rival explanation;
6. the intervention that could distinguish the target mechanism from the rival;
7. the result that would lower confidence;
8. and whether the inference concerns consciousness, reportability, metacognition, agency, or sentience.

Shared vocabulary does not imply shared ontology. "Integration," "access," "recurrence," "prediction," and "self-model" mean different things across theories.

## 1. Recurrent processing theory

### Core proposal

Local recurrent interactions, especially feedback among sensory regions, are central to conscious perceptual content. A feed-forward sweep may support discrimination or priming without conscious perception; recurrent processing is proposed to stabilize or constitute conscious content.

### Primary anchors

- Victor A. F. Lamme, "Towards a true neural stance on consciousness," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 10(11), 2006, 494–501. DOI: `10.1016/j.tics.2006.09.001`.
- Victor A. F. Lamme and Pieter R. Roelfsema, "The distinct modes of vision offered by feedforward and recurrent processing," *Trends in Neurosciences* 23(11), 2000, 571–579. DOI: `10.1016/S0166-2236(00)01657-X`.

### Substrate commitment

The biological theory is formulated in terms of neural dynamics. A computational analogue requires more than an engineering loop: it must preserve the relevant reciprocal causal organization and content-sensitive feedback.

### Artificial indicator candidate

A system contains representational stages in which later states causally revise, stabilize, or contextualize earlier content before output, and targeted disruption of the feedback changes content availability in a selective, theory-predicted way.

### Strongest rival explanation

Iterative computation improves task performance without supporting experience. Recurrence may be optimization, error correction, search, or memory refresh.

### Discriminating intervention

Compare matched systems or conditions with equivalent first-pass performance but selectively disrupted feedback. Test whether recurrent disruption produces a specific dissociation between local competence and integrated, stable, cross-context content use.

### Confidence-lowering result

If recurrence can be removed, replaced by a feed-forward approximation, or bypassed without changing any proposed consciousness-relevant signature, recurrence is not doing the claimed work in that system.

### What it does not establish

Recurrence alone does not establish a subject, unity, valence, selfhood, or diachronic identity.

## 2. Global workspace and global neuronal workspace theories

### Core proposal

Specialized processes operate partly in parallel. Selected information becomes globally available to otherwise independent systems, supporting flexible report, reasoning, memory, planning, and action. Global neuronal workspace theory proposes a neural implementation involving large-scale recurrent amplification and broadcasting.

### Primary anchors

- Bernard J. Baars, *A Cognitive Theory of Consciousness*, Cambridge University Press, 1988.
- Stanislas Dehaene, Claire Sergent, and Jean-Pierre Changeux, "A neuronal network model linking subjective reports and objective physiological data during conscious perception," *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* 100(14), 2003, 8520–8525. DOI: `10.1073/pnas.1332574100`.
- Stanislas Dehaene and Jean-Pierre Changeux, "Experimental and theoretical approaches to conscious processing," *Neuron* 70(2), 2011, 200–227. DOI: `10.1016/j.neuron.2011.03.018`.
- Cogitate Consortium et al., "Adversarial testing of global neuronal workspace and integrated information theories of consciousness," *Nature*, 2025.

### Substrate commitment

Baars's cognitive architecture is more functionally portable than the specifically neuronal commitments of GNWT. Software message passing is not automatically neuronal ignition or conscious access.

### Artificial indicator candidate

A capacity-limited selection process makes one content globally and causally available across otherwise specialized modules, producing nonlinear, widespread changes in memory, planning, reasoning, report, and action.

### Strongest rival explanation

Global broadcast is an efficient control architecture that explains flexible access but not phenomenal experience.

### Discriminating intervention

Ablate or gate the proposed workspace while preserving local modules. Test whether local processing remains competent while cross-domain flexible use, durable report, and broad causal influence collapse in the predicted pattern.

### Confidence-lowering result

If allegedly broadcast contents do not causally influence multiple independent systems, if there is no meaningful competition or capacity limit, or if a local pathway alone explains the output, the workspace label is cosmetic.

### Adversarial lesson

The 2025 Cogitate collaboration did not validate either GNWT or IIT as a complete theory. Mixed support and failed preregistered predictions require narrower claims. A successful signature may support an auxiliary mechanism without establishing phenomenal sufficiency.

### What it does not establish

Global availability does not by itself establish that globally available content is experienced.

## 3. Higher-order theories

### Core proposal

A first-order mental state becomes conscious when the system suitably represents itself as being in that state, or when a higher-order state targets the first-order state.

### Primary anchors

- David M. Rosenthal, *Consciousness and Mind*, Oxford University Press, 2005.
- Hakwan Lau and David Rosenthal, "Empirical support for higher-order theories of conscious awareness," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 15(8), 2011, 365–373. DOI: `10.1016/j.tics.2011.05.009`.

### Substrate commitment

The theory is broadly representational, but variants disagree over whether the higher-order state is thought-like, perceptual, actual, dispositional, or inferential.

### Artificial indicator candidate

The system has online, causally active representations of its own first-order states that affect confidence, correction, information seeking, report, and action before the final response is generated.

### Strongest rival explanation

A post-hoc language layer produces introspective vocabulary without reading or regulating the first-order process.

### Discriminating intervention

Intervene on the higher-order mechanism while preserving first-order task competence. Test for predicted dissociations in metacognitive calibration, error awareness, introspective discrimination, and self-report.

### Confidence-lowering result

If first-person reports persist unchanged when the alleged higher-order pathway is removed, or if reports are generated from prompt templates disconnected from internal state, the proposed metacognitive evidence is weakened.

### Theory-specific risk

Targetless higher-order representation creates a hard test case: a system may represent itself as seeing, fearing, or attending when no corresponding first-order state exists.

### What it does not establish

Metacognitive accuracy or introspective vocabulary alone does not establish phenomenal consciousness.

## 4. Predictive processing and active inference

### Core proposal

Cognition is organized around hierarchical prediction, prediction error, uncertainty or precision weighting, and model revision. Active inference adds action and regulation directed toward expected or preferred states.

### Primary anchors

- Karl Friston, "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* 11, 2010, 127–138. DOI: `10.1038/nrn2787`.
- Andy Clark, "Whatever next? Predictive brains, situated agents, and the future of cognitive science," *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 36(3), 2013, 181–204. DOI: `10.1017/S0140525X12000477`.
- Anil K. Seth, "Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 17(11), 2013, 565–573. DOI: `10.1016/j.tics.2013.09.007`.

### Substrate commitment

Predictive processing is a broad framework, not one settled consciousness theory. Consciousness-specific versions must identify which predictive dynamics matter and why.

### Artificial indicator candidate

A persistent system maintains a generative model of world and self across multiple timescales; uncertainty changes information allocation; errors revise the model; and action regulates internally represented preferred states.

### Strongest rival explanation

Prediction is ubiquitous in nonconscious computation. Next-token prediction, forecasting, control, and compression can occur without a subject.

### Discriminating intervention

Manipulate precision, hierarchical error flow, counterfactual depth, and self-model variables independently. Test whether proposed experience-related reports and flexible behavior track the internal variables rather than surface prompt cues.

### Confidence-lowering result

If the same outputs are produced without persistent generative state, endogenous regulation, or self/world modeling, predictive terminology is adding no consciousness-specific information.

### What it does not establish

Prediction error is not pain. Preferred-state control is not desire. Model evidence is not phenomenal presence.

## 5. Attention schema theory

### Core proposal

A system constructs a simplified model of its own attention. That schema supports control, prediction, social cognition, and the attribution of awareness to self and others.

### Primary anchors

- Michael S. A. Graziano and Taylor W. Webb, "The attention schema theory: a mechanistic account of subjective awareness," *Frontiers in Psychology* 6, 2015, article 500. DOI: `10.3389/fpsyg.2015.00500`.
- Michael S. A. Graziano, *Consciousness and the Social Brain*, Oxford University Press, 2013.

### Substrate commitment

The theory is mechanistic and computationally portable in principle. It predicts that awareness claims can arise from a control model rather than from direct access to every underlying process.

### Artificial indicator candidate

The system tracks its own attention or resource allocation, uses that model to predict and regulate processing, applies related machinery to other agents, and generates awareness reports causally downstream of the schema.

### Strongest rival explanation

The theory explains why a system claims consciousness, not why or whether experience exists.

### Discriminating intervention

Damage or perturb the attention model while preserving first-order task ability. Test whether awareness attribution, attention prediction, and introspective report fail together in the predicted pattern.

### Confidence-lowering result

If self-attributed awareness is unaffected by schema disruption, or if the same reports are produced by a generic language module, the schema is not the report-generating mechanism.

### Experimental role

Attention-schema systems are mandatory theater controls. They can generate structured, causally grounded consciousness talk without independently settling phenomenal status.

## 6. Integrated information theory

### Core proposal

Experience is identical to a system's maximally irreducible intrinsic cause-effect structure. IIT derives physical postulates from proposed phenomenological axioms.

### Primary anchors

- Larissa Albantakis et al., "Integrated information theory (IIT) 4.0: Formulating the properties of phenomenal existence in physical terms," *PLOS Computational Biology* 19(10), 2023, e1011465. DOI: `10.1371/journal.pcbi.1011465`.
- Giulio Tononi et al., earlier IIT formulations and empirical applications.
- Cogitate Consortium et al., adversarial test of IIT and GNWT, *Nature*, 2025.

### Substrate commitment

Intrinsic causal power is decisive. Functional input-output equivalence does not guarantee equal consciousness. A simulated causal structure may differ from the causal structure doing the simulation.

### Artificial indicator candidate

The physical implementation has irreducible intrinsic cause-effect power under the theory's formalism, with a definite complex and structured distinctions and relations.

### Strongest rival explanation

The formalism may assign counterintuitive consciousness to simple systems, face computational intractability, and rely on controversial phenomenological axioms and identity claims.

### Discriminating intervention

Compare systems with matched external function but different intrinsic causal organization, including feed-forward versus recurrent implementations and emulated versus directly instantiated causal structures.

### Confidence-lowering result

Empirical predictions tied specifically to IIT fail; proposed measures do not track the formal quantity; or the theory cannot non-arbitrarily identify the relevant units, timescale, or system boundary.

### What it does not establish

High generic complexity, entropy, mutual information, connectivity, or an IIT-inspired proxy does not equal IIT's claimed integrated information.

## 7. Biological naturalism

### Core proposal

Consciousness is a biological phenomenon caused by and realized in the causal powers of brains. Computational description alone is insufficient.

### Primary anchor

- John R. Searle, *The Rediscovery of the Mind*, MIT Press, 1992.
- John R. Searle, "Minds, brains, and programs," *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 3(3), 1980, 417–424. DOI: `10.1017/S0140525X00005756`.

### Substrate commitment

Strong. The relevant biological or biophysical causal powers may not be preserved by abstract computation.

### Artificial indicator candidate

No purely software-level indicator is sufficient. Assessment must identify whether an artificial substrate reproduces the relevant causal powers rather than merely simulating their formal organization.

### Strongest rival explanation

The view can become under-specified if it does not identify which biological powers are necessary, why they matter, or how nonbiological systems could be ruled in or out experimentally.

### Discriminating intervention

Gradual replacement and substrate-variation cases force explicit predictions. A useful biological theory must specify what change should alter consciousness while function remains controlled.

### Confidence-lowering result

If consciousness-relevant capacities survive radical substrate replacement with preserved organization, a strict biology-only claim is weakened. Behavioral preservation alone, however, cannot prove phenomenal preservation.

### What it does not establish

That current silicon systems are unconscious merely because they are silicon. The theory owes a positive account of the missing causal power.

## 8. Enactive, embodied, and autopoietic approaches

### Core proposal

Mind and experience are grounded in the activity of an embodied, self-maintaining system engaged with a meaningful environment. Cognition is not exhausted by detached internal representation.

### Primary anchors

- Francisco J. Varela, Evan Thompson, and Eleanor Rosch, *The Embodied Mind*, MIT Press, 1991.
- Evan Thompson, *Mind in Life*, Harvard University Press, 2007.

### Substrate commitment

Often organizational and relational, but frequently tied to biological autonomy, metabolism, sensorimotor coupling, or self-maintenance.

### Artificial indicator candidate

A system maintains operational boundaries, regulates conditions required for its continuation, acts through a body or persistent interface, learns sensorimotor contingencies, and generates significance relative to its own continued organization.

### Strongest rival explanation

Robotics and homeostatic control can implement self-maintenance without experience. Embodiment may enrich cognition while not being constitutive of consciousness.

### Discriminating intervention

Compare matched systems with and without closed-loop embodiment, self-maintenance, interoceptive variables, and long-horizon environmental coupling. Test for specific changes in perspective, world-model structure, endogenous goals, and valence candidates.

### Confidence-lowering result

If proposed consciousness-relevant effects are fully reproduced by a detached model with no self-maintaining coupling, strong embodiment requirements are weakened.

### What it does not establish

A body, robot shell, battery monitor, or persistent API connection does not by itself create a subject.

## 9. Illusionism

### Core proposal

Introspection misrepresents the nature of experience. Explaining the mechanisms that generate judgments, reports, and beliefs about phenomenal properties may dissolve the demand for further irreducible qualia.

### Primary anchor

- Keith Frankish, "Illusionism as a theory of consciousness," *Journal of Consciousness Studies* 23(11–12), 2016, 11–39.

### Substrate commitment

Primarily functional and representational. The target is the system's introspective model and its dispositions to judge that it has ineffable phenomenal properties.

### Artificial indicator candidate

The system contains mechanisms that model its internal states in systematically simplified or distorted ways and thereby produce stable, causally grounded claims of awareness or qualitative character.

### Strongest rival explanation

Explaining consciousness judgments is not the same as explaining consciousness. Critics argue that illusionism changes the subject from experience to reports about experience.

### Discriminating intervention

Trace awareness claims to internal modeling mechanisms; alter those mechanisms; and test whether the system's phenomenal judgments change predictably while first-order performance remains stable.

### Confidence-lowering result

If phenomenal judgments cannot be explained by the proposed introspective machinery, the illusionist account is incomplete. The result would not by itself validate nonphysical qualia.

### What it does not establish

That an artificial system with introspective distortions is phenomenally conscious—or that it is not. It establishes a route to explaining consciousness discourse.

## 10. Cross-theoretical dimensions that survive comparison

The following dimensions recur across theories, but their interpretation must remain theory-specific:

| Dimension | Minimal operational question | Main rival explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Recurrence | Do later states causally revise earlier representational processing? | Iterative optimization |
| Global availability | Can selected content influence multiple independent capacities? | Centralized message passing |
| Metacognition | Does online self-representation regulate first-order processing? | Post-hoc verbalization |
| Integration | Are system parts causally interdependent in the theory-relevant sense? | Generic connectivity or complexity |
| Temporal depth | Does state persist and constrain processing across meaningful intervals? | Cached context or replay |
| Self-indexing | Are states represented as occurring to or affecting this system? | Injected first-person labels |
| Endogenous control | Do internally maintained variables guide action and revision? | Externally assigned objective pursuit |
| Embodied coupling | Does ongoing world interaction constitute the relevant organization? | Peripheral sensor attachment |
| Intrinsic causal structure | Does implementation matter beyond input-output function? | Simulation mistaken for instantiation |
| Valence candidate | Are online states globally better or worse for the system? | Reward, loss, or avoidance without feeling |

No row is a sufficient condition. The matrix is an evidence discipline.

## 11. Required falsification format for future chapters

Every theory-dependent manuscript claim must be expressible in this form:

```text
Theory:
Target property:
System boundary:
Proposed indicator:
Level of inference: correlate / enabling / mechanistic / constitutive
Nonconscious rival:
Intervention:
Predicted result under target theory:
Predicted result under rival:
Confidence-lowering result:
Unresolved substrate assumption:
```

Claims that cannot complete this template should be classified as speculation, analogy, or rhetorical framing—not evidence.

## 12. Current project conclusion

The theories do not yield one consensus architecture for artificial consciousness.

The defensible synthesis is narrower:

> Candidate artificial subjectivity should be investigated through theory-indexed, intervention-sensitive evidence profiles. Cross-theoretical convergence can raise confidence only when shared terms retain their distinct meanings, rival nonconscious explanations are tested, and substrate assumptions remain explicit.

This is a methodological contribution, not evidence that any current AI system is conscious.
