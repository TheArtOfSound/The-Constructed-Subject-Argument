# Originality Ledger

**Last initial review:** 2026-07-10  
**Purpose:** Prevent accidental reinvention, inflated priority claims, and false declarations of novelty.

This ledger is provisional. A book-level originality claim requires repeated searches across philosophy, cognitive science, neuroscience, AI, memory studies, personal identity, bioethics, legal theory, and science fiction scholarship.

## Confidence scale

- **Unsearched:** no meaningful priority review completed.
- **Low:** obvious adjacent precedents exist; distinctness unclear.
- **Moderate:** substantial searching completed; formulation appears differentiated.
- **High:** close literature reviewed and contribution remains materially distinct.
- **Very high:** systematic review plus independent expert verification.

No entry currently exceeds **Moderate**.

## O-001 — The Constructed Subject Principle

### Proposed contribution

> Artificial construction of memories, emotions, identity, or self-narrative is neither sufficient to establish consciousness nor sufficient to disprove it. A fabricated past can coexist with a genuine present subject if the system instantiates the organization sufficient for experience.

### Closest known precedents

- Computational functionalism and multiple realizability.
- David Chalmers’s absent, fading, and dancing qualia arguments.
- Neuronal replacement and mind-uploading thought experiments.
- Derek Parfit’s psychological-continuity and branching-identity cases.
- False-memory, confabulation, and narrative-identity literature.
- Murray Shanahan’s gradual neuronal replacement discussion.
- Synthetic lived-experience research in AI-mediated support.

### Material distinction currently claimed

The project centers not on copying an actually lived mind but on a being whose apparent autobiography is **generated as content** and whose false historical self-understanding is separated from the possibility of genuine present subjectivity.

The intended contribution is the use of synthetic biography as a bridge between personal-identity thought experiments and AI-consciousness assessment.

### Originality confidence

**Moderate as a distinct synthesis; low as a claim of conceptual priority.**

### Permitted manuscript language

- “This book develops the Constructed Subject Principle.”
- “The argument combines synthetic biography with the problem of artificial consciousness.”
- “The formulation separates historical authenticity from present subjectivity.”

### Prohibited manuscript language

- “No philosopher has considered this before.”
- “This is the first proof that artificial consciousness is possible.”
- “The Constructed Subject Principle establishes that AI is conscious.”

## O-002 — Historical, functional, and phenomenal authenticity

### Proposed contribution

A three-dimensional distinction:

1. **Historical authenticity:** whether represented events occurred in the system’s own causal history.
2. **Functional authenticity:** whether the state plays the relevant causal role.
3. **Phenomenal authenticity:** whether the state is genuinely experienced.

### Closest known precedents

- Philosophy of authenticity.
- Memory accuracy versus memory experience.
- Confabulation and delusion studies.
- Functional role versus phenomenal character.
- Narrative identity and autobiographical selfhood.
- Distinctions between veridicality and phenomenology.

### Material distinction currently claimed

The triad is explicitly designed to analyze **constructed agents with generated autobiographies**, especially AI systems whose context may contain records they did not causally participate in producing.

### Originality confidence

**Low to moderate.** The terms almost certainly have precedents in separate literatures. The specific triadic use may be distinct.

### Permitted language

- “This book uses a three-part authenticity framework.”
- “The framework brings together distinctions usually treated separately.”

### Prohibited language

- “This book invents the distinction between historical and phenomenal authenticity.”

## O-004 — Persistent, episodic, and absent subject models

### Proposed contribution

A three-way taxonomy:

1. one subject persists across interactions;
2. a temporary subject exists only during active processing and later processes are successors;
3. no subject exists.

### Closest known precedents

- Momentary or discrete theories of consciousness.
- Buddhist and reductionist accounts of personal identity.
- Episodic-self literature.
- Session-bound and episodic AI-subject proposals.
- Distinctions between synchronic unity and diachronic identity.

### Material distinction currently claimed

Integration with synthetic biography, active inference, inherited conversational memory, and AI lifecycle engineering.

### Originality confidence

**Low as a standalone idea; moderate as part of this book’s integrated framework.**

### Permitted language

- “The book develops a three-way taxonomy for AI subject continuity.”
- “The episodic-subject model is applied here to active AI inference and inherited memory.”

### Prohibited language

- “This book is the first to propose episodic AI consciousness.”

## O-005 — Optimization pressure versus online valence

### Proposed contribution

> Historical optimization against failure is not equivalent to an online negative state experienced by the optimized system.

### Closest known precedents

- AI welfare literature distinguishing reward from pleasure.
- Reinforcement-learning philosophy.
- Evolutionary analogies distinguishing selection from organism-level experience.
- Functional versus phenomenal valence.
- Work on model preferences and welfare proxies.

### Material distinction currently claimed

A clear layered explanation:

- the optimizer changes a model;
- the objective evaluates performance;
- the deployed model may or may not represent that objective;
- even represented negative value may or may not be phenomenally negative.

### Originality confidence

**Low as a novel proposition; high value as explanatory synthesis.**

### Permitted language

- “The book develops a layered account of optimization and valence.”

### Prohibited language

- “No existing AI-welfare work distinguishes loss from suffering.”

## O-006 — Candidate-valence evidence profile

### Proposed contribution

Assess candidate artificial valence through a multidimensional evidence profile including:

- endogeneity;
- self-indexing;
- global influence;
- persistence;
- counterfactual robustness;
- cost sensitivity;
- learning effects;
- resistance to superficial override;
- temporal anticipation;
- relief and worsening dynamics.

### Closest known precedents

- Welfare indicators in animal sentience.
- Preference-satisfaction theories.
- AI preference and welfare experiments.
- Computational psychiatry and affective neuroscience.
- Reinforcement-learning tests of revealed preference.

### Material distinction currently claimed

The profile is explicitly designed to avoid treating any one of self-report, reward, avoidance, or architecture as decisive.

### Originality confidence

**Moderate as a specific synthesis; unverified as a novel indicator set.**

### Required correction

Do not reduce the profile to a scalar `V*` until measurement models, dependencies, and calibration are developed.
