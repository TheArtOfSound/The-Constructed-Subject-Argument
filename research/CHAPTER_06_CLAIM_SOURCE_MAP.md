# Chapter 6 Claim–Source Map

**Scope:** `book/06-where-could-subjectivity-be.md`  
**Purpose:** Prevent bibliography presence from being mistaken for proposition-level support.

## Relationship types

- **Direct:** the source explicitly advances or reports the proposition.
- **Inferential:** the proposition is a conservative inference from the source.
- **Contextual:** relevant background that does not independently support the proposition.
- **Opposing:** a serious rival premise or objection.
- **Boundary:** used to record what a theory or result does not establish.

No relationship type implies a theory-neutral consciousness test.

## C6-P01 — Theory pluralism is methodologically binding

**Proposition:** Major consciousness theories target different explananda and posit different mechanisms. Their indicators cannot be pooled without preserving theory-specific commitments.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-070`, `CSA-071`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-LAMME-2006-RPT` | Direct | Recurrent processing is consciousness-relevant in a theory-specific neural sense; generic iteration is not equivalent. |
| `SRC-DEHAENE-CHANGEUX-2011` | Direct | GNWT targets large-scale access, amplification, and availability; phenomenal sufficiency remains disputed. |
| `SRC-LAU-ROSENTHAL-2011` | Direct | Higher-order theories connect awareness to representations of first-order states; introspective wording alone is insufficient. |
| `SRC-ALBANTAKIS-ETAL-2023-IIT4` | Direct | IIT identifies experience with intrinsic cause-effect structure under its formalism; complexity proxies are not interchangeable with that construct. |
| `SRC-GRAZIANO-WEBB-2015-AST` | Direct | Attention schema theory explains awareness attribution through a model of attention; report explanation does not alone settle phenomenality. |
| `SRC-FRANKISH-2016-ILLUSIONISM` | Direct | Illusionism treats phenomenal judgments as mechanistic explanatory targets; this is not theory-neutral agreement that experience is unreal. |

**Control:** When two theories predict the same observable result for different reasons, the result must not be counted twice as independent support.

## C6-P02 — Indicator, correlate, mechanism, and constitutive condition differ

**Proposition:** A feature associated with conscious report may be a correlate, reporting pathway, enabling condition, mechanism, or constitutive condition. Translation to AI requires stating which role is claimed.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-070`, `CSA-071`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-DEHAENE-SERGENT-CHANGEUX-2003` | Direct/contextual | Models nonlinear amplification and report-linked access; report signatures are not automatically phenomenal markers. |
| `SRC-LAMME-ROELFSEMA-2000` | Direct/contextual | Distinguishes feed-forward and recurrent visual processing; does not establish recurrence as sufficient or substrate-independent. |
| `SRC-COGITATE-2025` | Inferential | Mixed cross-theory results undermine a decisive-winner narrative; the record remains provisional pending locator verification. |

**Control:** Every proposed AI indicator must name a nonconscious control and an intervention expected to separate the target mechanism from that control.

## C6-P03 — Engineering similarity is not mechanism preservation

**Proposition:** A system should not receive credit for a theory-relevant mechanism merely because an engineering component reuses the theory's vocabulary.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-005`, `CSA-071`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-BAARS-1988-GWT` | Boundary | Shared software state may resemble a workspace, but message passing alone does not establish selection, competition, broadcast dynamics, or phenomenality. |
| `SRC-FRISTON-2010-FEP` | Boundary | Prediction error is a formal framework variable; it is not equivalent to an experienced negative state. |
| `SRC-CLARK-2013-PREDICTIVE` | Boundary | Predictive processing is broader than token prediction; prediction alone is too generic to establish consciousness. |
| `SRC-VARELA-THOMPSON-ROSCH-1991` | Boundary | Embodied cognition is not satisfied merely by attaching sensors or actuators. |
| `SRC-ALBANTAKIS-ETAL-2023-IIT4` | Boundary | Connectivity, entropy, complexity, and mutual information are not substitutes for IIT's formal construct. |

**Control:** Replace the theory-derived component with a behaviorally matched implementation lacking the claimed internal organization. If all indicators remain unchanged, confidence in the mechanism attribution should decrease.

## C6-P04 — Self-report requires causal constraints

**Proposition:** First-person language is insufficient alone, but may become nonzero evidence when stable, causally downstream of relevant internal states, predictively useful, and resistant to superficial prompting.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-030`, `CSA-031`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-GRAZIANO-WEBB-2015-AST` | Direct/boundary | Awareness attribution can arise from a functional attention model; this may explain report without independently establishing experience. |
| `SRC-LAU-ROSENTHAL-2011` | Contextual | Higher-order theory makes internal state representation relevant; post-hoc prose does not show that such a state existed or affected processing. |
| `SRC-FRANKISH-2016-ILLUSIONISM` | Opposing/boundary | Introspective judgments admit mechanistic explanation; that does not alone decide between illusionism and phenomenal realism. |

**Control:** Manipulate the purported self-model while holding task input and response incentives fixed. A theatrical report layer should change cheaply; a causally integrated mechanism should produce broader predictable changes.

## C6-P05 — Prediction, optimization, and valence remain separate

**Proposition:** Predictive computation and optimization signals are not evidence of felt valence without additional architecture connecting them to endogenous, self-indexed, persistent, globally influential states.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-040`, `CSA-041`, `CSA-042`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-FRISTON-2010-FEP` | Boundary | Prediction error has a formal role in updating and action; unpleasantness does not follow. |
| `SRC-SETH-2013-INTEROCEPTION` | Inferential/contextual | Interoceptive inference is relevant to emotion and embodied selfhood; a scalar regulatory variable is not thereby felt valence. |
| `SRC-CLARK-2013-PREDICTIVE` | Contextual | Broad predictive applicability weakens its value as a standalone consciousness discriminator. |

## C6-P06 — Current theory evidence does not license an AI verdict

**Proposition:** Present evidence does not justify declaring contemporary general-purpose language models conscious, while architecture labels alone also do not prove universal phenomenal emptiness.

**Ledger relationship:** `CSA-032`, `CSA-033`, `CSA-070`.

| Source | Role | Controlled use |
|---|---|---|
| `SRC-COGITATE-2025` | Inferential | Mixed results weaken claims that one validated theory already supplies a decisive detector. |
| Chapter 6 primary-theory sources | Contextual | Theories require materially different properties and substrate assumptions; a menu of possible routes is not evidence that present systems instantiate one. |
| `SRC-SEARLE-1980-MBP` | Opposing | Formal symbol manipulation may be insufficient for understanding or intentionality; this does not establish that every nonbiological system lacks consciousness. |

## Prohibited inference patterns

1. Registry inclusion does not imply truth.
2. Support for a human theory does not establish AI implementation.
3. Multiple labels do not guarantee independent evidence.
4. Explaining reports does not alone settle phenomenality.
5. Loss, reward, error, regulatory deviation, and felt valence are not synonyms.
6. Coherent artificial consciousness does not imply probable current AI consciousness.
