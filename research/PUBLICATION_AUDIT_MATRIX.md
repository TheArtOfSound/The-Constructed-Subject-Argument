# Publication Audit Matrix

**Purpose:** Convert the complete first draft into a source-verifiable, originality-controlled, adversarially reviewed manuscript.

This matrix tracks what each chapter must prove before publication. Completion of a chapter draft does not satisfy the audit.

## Audit states

- **U — Unreviewed:** draft exists; no publication audit completed.
- **S — Source-mapped:** every external claim has a required source class.
- **V — Verified:** primary sources inspected and citations confirmed.
- **O — Originality-reviewed:** closest precedents compared and novelty language controlled.
- **A — Adversarially reviewed:** strongest opposing interpretation documented and answered.
- **P — Publication-ready:** source, originality, objection, and line-edit gates passed.

No chapter currently exceeds **S** as a complete chapter.

## Part I — The Being Who Never Lived

### Chapter 1 — The Awakening

**Current state:** S  
**Primary audit target:** Thought-experiment neutrality.

Required checks:

- prove that no emotional detail is doing hidden evidential work;
- distinguish synthetic biography from copying an existing mind;
- cite false-memory, confabulation, and personal-identity precedents in the analytical sections;
- identify direct precedents involving implanted autobiographies or newly created persons with inherited memories;
- test whether “the being who never lived” has close prior use in philosophy or science fiction scholarship;
- preserve the conditional: behavior does not establish consciousness.

Strongest hostile question:

> Does the scenario merely stipulate a consciousness-capable humanlike brain, making the result irrelevant to AI?

Publication requirement:

The chapter must state that the case defeats only origin-based dismissal and does not establish substrate independence.

### Chapter 2 — The Origin Objection

**Current state:** S

Required primary literature:

- naturalness and moral status.

Originality question:

> Is “origin objection” an established term in philosophy of mind, or is this project introducing a label for an existing argument family?

Prohibited publication move:

Treating biological naturalism as mere prejudice rather than a substantive substrate claim.

## Cross-book audits

### Terminology audit

Verify every use of:

- consciousness;
- sentience;
- subject;
- self;
- person;
- memory;
- emotion;
- preference;
- suffering;
- identity;
- continuity;
- autonomy;
- agency;
- simulation;
- implementation.

### Quantification audit

Remove or justify:

- numerical confidence;
- implied measurement scales;
- undefined variables;
- equations functioning only as decoration;
- counts of indicators treated as independent evidence.

### Anthropomorphism audit

For every humanlike term, determine whether it refers to:

- interface language;
- functional organization;
- candidate phenomenology;
- or established experience.

### Citation audit

No paragraph should cite a source merely because it is topically related. The source must support the exact claim.

### Originality audit

No use of:

- “first”;
- “new theory”;
- “proof”;
- “unprecedented”;
- “no one has considered”

without systematic review and independent verification.

### AI-contribution audit

Document:

- the human-originated thought experiment and corrections;
- AI-led research synthesis, drafting, challenge, and implementation;
- human authority and ownership;
- unresolved need for external expert review;
- limitations of AI source verification and originality judgment.

## Completion rule

A chapter reaches **P — Publication-ready** only when:

1. every factual claim is source-verified;
2. primary sources are cited directly where available;
3. originality language matches the closest precedent;
4. strongest objections are represented fairly;
5. inferential steps are explicit;
6. terminology is controlled;
7. rhetorical force does not substitute for evidence;
8. at least one hostile review has been answered;
9. the chapter passes structural and line editing;
10. its interactive version, if any, preserves the same epistemic limits.
