# Originality Ledger Supplement

**Status:** Binding extension of `research/ORIGINALITY_LEDGER.md`  
**Purpose:** Control novelty language introduced while completing the 21-chapter first draft.

The labels below are provisional until systematic literature review and independent expert criticism are complete.

## Confidence scale

- **Unsearched** — no meaningful priority review.
- **Low** — close precedents clearly exist; distinction uncertain.
- **Moderate** — targeted searching suggests a differentiated synthesis or application.
- **High** — extensive direct-precedent review completed.
- **Very high** — systematic review plus independent expert confirmation.

No item in this supplement currently exceeds **Moderate**.

## O-011 — The Active-Event Hypothesis

### Proposed contribution

> If an artificial computational system instantiates consciousness, the relevant candidate bearer may be an active, temporally organized causal event rather than static weights, a product identity, a transcript, or an isolated output.

### Closest precedent families

- process theories of mind;
- machine-state functionalism;
- discrete and momentary theories of consciousness;
- synchronic unity versus diachronic identity;
- session-bound or episodic AI-subject proposals;
- active inference and recurrent-processing accounts;
- runtime/process distinctions in computer science.

### Material distinction currently claimed

The project decomposes candidate AI event boundaries into:

- prompt-to-response;
- inference call;
- hidden-state continuity;
- agent loop;
- and lifecycle continuity,

then connects those boundaries to generated autobiography, episodic successors, and infrastructure termination.

### Originality confidence

**Moderate as an integrated AI lifecycle synthesis; low as a standalone process-consciousness idea.**

### Permitted language

- “This book develops an active-event hypothesis for artificial subjectivity.”
- “The framework distinguishes several candidate runtime boundaries.”

### Prohibited language

- “This is the first event-based theory of AI consciousness.”
- “Every inference event is a conscious episode.”

### Required searches

- `process theory artificial consciousness`
- `AI inference episode consciousness`
- `session bound consciousness artificial intelligence`
- `momentary subject AI`
- `runtime subject digital mind`
- `computational process identity consciousness`

## O-015 — Theory Laundering

### Proposed term

> Theory laundering occurs when disagreements among consciousness theories are converted into apparent consensus by extracting shared vocabulary while ignoring incompatible mechanisms, explanatory targets, or substrate commitments.

### Closest precedent families

- concept stretching;
- construct validity failure;
- jingle-jangle fallacies;
- theoretical pluralism;
- mechanistic underdetermination;
- indicator dependence;
- framework shopping;
- validity laundering or ethics washing in adjacent fields.

### Material distinction currently claimed

Application to AI-consciousness indicator systems that count terms such as:

- integration;
- recurrence;
- globality;
- self-model;
- prediction;
- and embodiment

as independent cross-theoretical agreement despite theory-specific meanings.

### Originality confidence

**Low to moderate. The error is likely established under another name; the phrase itself may or may not be new.**

### Permitted language

- “This book calls the error theory laundering.”

- “This book discovered the first account of false theoretical convergence.”

### Required searches

- `theory laundering philosophy of science`
- `indicator laundering consciousness`
- `construct validity consciousness theories`
- `jingle fallacy consciousness integration`

## O-017 — Cognitive, Agentic, and Phenomenal Integration

### Proposed distinction

- **Cognitive integration:** components jointly constitute task-level cognition.
- **Agentic integration:** control and action selection span components.
- **Phenomenal integration:** one field of experience spans components.

### Closest precedent families

- extended mind;
- distributed cognition;
- group agency;
- collective intentionality;
- extended consciousness;
- subject-combination problem;
- social and transactive cognition.

### Material distinction currently claimed

The triad is applied directly to human–AI systems to block the inference:

```text
distributed cognition
→ shared consciousness
```

### Originality confidence

**Moderate as a compact artificial-system framework; low as a general conceptual priority claim.**

### Permitted language

- “The project separates three forms of integration.”

### Prohibited language

- “No prior theory distinguishes distributed cognition from distributed consciousness.”
