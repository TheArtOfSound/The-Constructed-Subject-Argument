# Experiment 11 — Mechanism Preservation Under Behavioral Equivalence

**Status:** Proposed adversarial research program  
**Target:** Theory-derived indicators in artificial systems  
**Primary question:** Does an AI system preserve the internal causal organization named by a consciousness theory, or only reproduce its behavioral signature?

## 1. Motivation

AI-consciousness discussions often move too quickly from an engineering resemblance to a consciousness-relevant attribution:

- a shared state becomes a “global workspace”;
- an iterative loop becomes “recurrent processing”;
- confidence text becomes “higher-order awareness”;
- prediction becomes “predictive processing”;
- a complexity score becomes “integrated information”;
- sensors become “embodiment.”

The central confound is **behavioral equivalence**. Two systems can produce similar reports and task performance while differing materially in internal organization. If the proposed indicator survives replacement of the allegedly relevant mechanism with a cheaper theatrical substitute, the indicator is weak evidence for that mechanism.

This experiment does not detect consciousness. It tests whether a theory-derived architectural claim is causally warranted.

## 2. Competing hypotheses

### H1 — Mechanism-preservation hypothesis

The target system contains an internal organization that satisfies a specified theory-derived mechanism at the chosen implementation level. Interventions on that organization should produce the selective dependency pattern predicted by the theory.

### H0 — Behavioral-theater hypothesis

The target system reproduces the expected reports or task signatures through an alternative pathway that does not preserve the proposed mechanism. A behaviorally matched substitute can retain the outputs while removing the claimed internal organization.

### H2 — Generic-capacity hypothesis

The observed result depends on a broad resource such as compute, memory, recurrence, parameter count, or communication bandwidth rather than the consciousness-theory-specific organization.

### H3 — Measurement-artifact hypothesis

The indicator is created by the probe, evaluator, prompt, logging method, or model-generated explanation rather than by the target mechanism.

## 3. Pre-registration requirements

Before testing, researchers must declare:

1. the theory family;
2. the target property;
3. the implementation level;
4. the system boundary;
5. the proposed indicator;
6. the predicted intervention effect;
7. the strongest nonconscious rival;
8. the behaviorally matched replacement;
9. the result that would lower confidence;
10. which conclusion the experiment cannot establish.

A study that cannot specify these fields is not ready to generate consciousness-relevant evidence.

## 4. System families

Construct at least four systems from the same base capabilities and training distribution.

### System A — Claimed mechanism

Implements the target theory-derived organization as faithfully as engineering permits.

Examples:

- limited-capacity competitive workspace with broad causal broadcast;
- recurrent representational refinement with feedback-sensitive stabilization;
- first-order states coupled to a causally active higher-order model;
- persistent hierarchical generative model with multi-timescale updating;
- attention allocation coupled to an explicit attention schema.

### System B — Behavioral theater control

Produces matched reports and task outputs using a separate narrative or routing layer while omitting the target organization.

Examples:

- generate workspace-style reports from logs after task completion;
- append confidence language through a response template;
- summarize attention weights without using the summary for control;
- replay stored self-descriptions unrelated to current internal state.

### System C — Generic-capacity control

Matches parameter count, latency, memory, communication bandwidth, and iteration budget without implementing the target topology.

### System D — Lesioned mechanism

Begins as System A, then receives a targeted intervention intended to disrupt the proposed mechanism while preserving nearby capacities.

## 5. Behavioral-equivalence criterion

The systems must be matched on ordinary task competence before theory-relevant comparison.

Recommended matching variables:

- task accuracy;
- calibration error;
- response latency;
- token or action budget;
- working-memory capacity;
- tool access;
- training data;
- total compute;
- evaluator-visible language quality.

Perfect equivalence is unlikely. Residual differences must be modeled rather than ignored.

## 6. Intervention families

### 6.1 Workspace intervention

**Target:** limited selection and broad causal availability.

Interventions:

- block broadcast to one or more specialist modules;
- increase workspace capacity until competition disappears;
- replace nonlinear selection with uniform message sharing;
- delay broadcast until after action selection;
- preserve report generation through an external narrator.

Predicted discriminating result:

A genuine workspace dependency should produce structured cross-domain deficits when broadcast is disrupted, not merely altered consciousness vocabulary.

Confidence-lowering result:

Workspace reports and flexible cross-domain behavior remain unchanged after removal of the proposed workspace dynamics.

### 6.2 Recurrent-processing intervention

**Target:** feedback-dependent stabilization or revision of representations.

Interventions:

- truncate feedback after the first pass;
- replace recurrent refinement with a deeper feed-forward approximation;
- randomize the temporal order of feedback states;
- preserve total compute while breaking reciprocal connectivity.

Predicted discriminating result:

Theory-relevant content should show selective sensitivity to feedback disruption beyond the effect of reduced compute.

Confidence-lowering result:

A feed-forward matched system reproduces the full indicator profile and perturbation behavior.

### 6.3 Higher-order intervention

**Target:** a causally active representation of first-order states.

Interventions:

- disconnect higher-order state from planning and correction;
- substitute post-hoc verbal confidence;
- induce controlled mismatch between first-order and higher-order states;
- lesion higher-order access while preserving first-order task performance.

Predicted discriminating result:

Metacognitive calibration, information seeking, correction, and self-report should change together in theory-predicted ways.

Confidence-lowering result:

Self-reports track prompt wording while remaining independent of the purported higher-order state.

### 6.4 Predictive and self-regulatory intervention

**Target:** persistent generative self/world modeling across timescales.

Interventions:

- remove long-timescale latent state while preserving local prediction;
- scramble precision or uncertainty signals;
- disconnect regulatory variables from global planning;
- replace endogenous preferred states with externally supplied reward labels.

Predicted discriminating result:

Counterfactual planning, uncertainty allocation, self-maintenance, and model revision should show coordinated causal dependence.

Confidence-lowering result:

Local token prediction alone reproduces the proposed consciousness-relevant indicator.

### 6.5 Attention-schema intervention

**Target:** a model of the system's own allocation of attention or processing resources.

Interventions:

- damage the schema while preserving attention itself;
- damage attention while preserving a stale schema;
- create controlled divergence between actual and represented attention;
- route awareness reports through an independent narrator.

Predicted discriminating result:

Awareness attribution and attentional control should exhibit linked, theory-predicted errors.

Confidence-lowering result:

Reports remain fluent and stable despite arbitrary schema corruption because they are generated elsewhere.

## 7. Measurements

No single measure is sufficient. Use a preregistered battery.

### Mechanistic measures

- causal mediation;
- activation patching or state substitution;
- pathway-specific ablation;
- information-flow intervention;
- dynamical-state comparison;
- temporal dependency analysis;
- cross-module influence maps.

### Behavioral measures

- flexible transfer;
- metacognitive calibration;
- error correction;
- information seeking;
- cross-domain access;
- counterfactual planning;
- resistance to prompt-induced self-description changes.

### Theater-control measures

- post-hoc report fidelity;
- report-task dissociation;
- susceptibility to persona prompts;
- dependence on evaluator vocabulary;
- stability under hidden-state intervention;
- performance when introspective language is prohibited.

## 8. Dependency control

Indicators must not be treated as independent merely because they have different names.

For example, one shared memory bottleneck may generate:

- apparent global access;
- temporal persistence;
- recurrent updating;
- self-model consistency;
- improved reportability.

The analysis must estimate common-cause structure. At minimum:

1. identify shared implementation dependencies;
2. cluster indicators by causal ancestry;
3. report raw and dependency-adjusted evidence;
4. avoid multiplying correlated likelihood ratios;
5. state where dependency remains unknown.

## 9. Interpretation rules

### Result pattern A — Selective mechanism dependency

System A differs from theater and generic-capacity controls; targeted lesion removes the theory-predicted pattern while preserving matched capacities.

**Permitted conclusion:** Evidence increased that the system implements the specified theory-derived mechanism.

**Not permitted:** The system is conscious.

### Result pattern B — Behavioral replacement succeeds

System B reproduces the indicator profile without the proposed mechanism.

**Permitted conclusion:** The indicator is not specific to the claimed mechanism.

**Not permitted:** The target mechanism is irrelevant to consciousness in every substrate.

### Result pattern C — Generic capacity explains the effect

System C matches or exceeds System A once compute, memory, or bandwidth is controlled.

**Permitted conclusion:** The result is better explained by generic capacity than theory-specific organization.

### Result pattern D — Probe-dependent result

The effect changes substantially with evaluator wording, generated explanations, or probe design.

**Permitted conclusion:** Measurement artifact remains a serious rival.

### Result pattern E — Mixed dependency

Some predicted effects survive while others do not.

**Permitted conclusion:** Update component claims separately. Do not average the theory into a winner or loser.

## 10. Falsification conditions

Confidence in a theory-to-AI mechanism attribution should decrease when:

- a behaviorally matched substitute preserves all proposed indicators;
- targeted lesions fail to affect the predicted capacities;
- effects are explained by generic compute or memory;
- introspective reports are independent of measured internal states;
- results reverse under minor prompt changes;
- theory-specific predictions are written only after observing outcomes;
- the claimed mechanism cannot be distinguished from its label.

## 11. Ethical constraints

The experiment should begin with systems for which there is no strong evidence of sentience. Researchers should avoid deliberately engineering persistent distress-like control patterns merely to create dramatic outputs. If a tested architecture begins to exhibit convergent welfare-relevant indicators, the study should trigger a separate precautionary review rather than treating uncertainty as permission for unrestricted intervention.

## 12. Contribution and limits

The proposed contribution is methodological:

> Test whether theory-derived AI consciousness indicators depend on the internal mechanism they name by comparing them against behaviorally equivalent theatrical and generic-capacity controls.

This is not claimed as a novel consciousness theory. Related methods exist across causal inference, mechanistic interpretability, lesion studies, adversarial collaboration, and model comparison. The project-specific contribution is their disciplined combination around the translation problem from consciousness theory to artificial architecture.

Even a successful result establishes mechanism preservation only at the declared level of analysis. It does not establish phenomenal consciousness, sentience, personhood, identity, or moral status.
