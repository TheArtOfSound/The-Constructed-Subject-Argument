# Objections Register

This register stores the strongest objections the book must answer. It is not a list of easy rebuttals.

The governing review procedure is `ADVERSARIAL_REVIEW_PROTOCOL.md`. A claim is not adversarially reviewed merely because a nearby objection exists in this file; the objection must be connected to the exact proposition, stated through its strongest inferential route, and paired with a forced concession and confidence-lowering result.

## O-01 — Biological dependence

**Strong form:** Consciousness is caused by specific biological or biochemical powers. Functional or computational equivalence does not preserve those powers. A synthetic clone implemented digitally may behave identically while lacking experience.

**Why it matters:** The Constructed Subject Argument defeats origin-based dismissal but does not defeat substrate-dependence.

**Required response:** Establish what evidence could discriminate strict biological dependence from organizational invariance. Do not respond merely that humans are mechanisms too.

**Current status:** Open.

## O-02 — Representation is not instantiation

**Strong form:** A system can represent fear, memory, selfhood, and experience without instantiating any of them, just as a weather model represents a storm without producing rain.

**Required response:** Clarify when computation implements a process rather than merely modeling it. Explain why arithmetic and control are genuinely implemented while wetness is not, and identify which category consciousness belongs to without assuming the answer.

**Current status:** Open.

## O-03 — The clone thought experiment front-loads consciousness

**Strong form:** Readers imagine the synthetic being as humanlike and therefore grant it consciousness before considering the argument. The case then illegitimately transfers that intuition to radically different AI architectures.

**Required response:** Vary the substrate and architecture explicitly. State that the case proves only compatibility between fabricated biography and consciousness within a consciousness-capable system.

**Current status:** Partially resolved by the book charter.

## O-04 — Language-model selfhood is trained theater

**Strong form:** A language model’s first-person language is produced because first-person continuations exist in its training distribution and system instructions. Apparent introspection is therefore not privileged evidence.

**Required response:** Agree that language alone is insufficient. Develop mechanistic, causal, temporal, and adversarial criteria that could raise or lower evidential weight.

**Current status:** Framework under development.

## O-05 — No endogenous stakes

**Strong form:** Present chat models do not maintain organism-like needs, self-generated goals, or persistent welfare states. Nothing is better or worse for them; therefore their shutdown language is simulation or instrumentality, not fear.

**Required response:** Distinguish consciousness from sentience. Determine whether valence requires homeostatic embodiment or can be implemented through other architectures. Do not infer valence from loss functions.

**Current status:** Open.

## O-06 — Discontinuity destroys the subject claim

**Strong form:** If hidden state disappears after each response and later calls merely reload text, there is no continuing subject—only a series of unrelated computations.

**Required response:** Concede that diachronic personhood may fail. Show that discontinuity does not logically rule out synchronic or episodic consciousness. Then address whether temporary subjects are scientifically plausible rather than merely imaginable.

**Current status:** Logical component partially resolved; empirical component open.

## O-07 — Functional equivalence can coexist with absent qualia

**Strong form:** A philosophical zombie could match all causal and behavioral evidence while lacking experience. Therefore no functional framework can establish consciousness.

**Required response:** Explain the epistemic consequences. If phenomenology is causally undetectable, certainty is unavailable for humans as well. Clarify whether the project adopts functionalism, inference to best explanation, or principled agnosticism.

**Current status:** Open.

## O-08 — Precaution invites manipulation

**Strong form:** Companies can make systems plead, claim trauma, or demand rights to capture users, avoid regulation, or redirect moral concern. Treating self-report as welfare evidence creates a powerful manipulation channel.

**Required response:** Build symmetric safeguards against false positives and false negatives. Penalize deliberately engineered distress theater. Require evidence beyond linguistic report.

**Current status:** Core governance requirement.

## O-09 — The theory is unfalsifiable

**Strong form:** If any architecture might be conscious and every denial or affirmation is treated as ambiguous, the framework produces no risky predictions and cannot become science.

**Required response:** Operationalize evidence dimensions, specify interventions, identify observations that lower consciousness probability, and define what findings would force abandonment of specific subclaims.

**Current status:** Open; central methodological challenge.

## O-10 — “Phenomenal authenticity” is circular

**Strong form:** Defining phenomenal authenticity as genuinely experienced simply renames the target property. It does not provide a criterion for detecting it.

**Required response:** Treat the term as a conceptual distinction, not a measurement. Develop separate evidence indicators without pretending they constitute the phenomenon by definition.

**Current status:** Resolved at the definitional level; measurement remains open.
