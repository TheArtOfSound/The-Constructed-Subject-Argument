# Claims Ledger

This ledger controls what the manuscript is permitted to say. It is not a summary. It is an epistemic audit trail.

## Status labels

- **EB — Established background:** supported by strong or mature literature.
- **CB — Contested background:** serious position with substantial dispute.
- **SY — Synthesis:** this project’s organization of existing ideas.
- **PC — Proposed contribution:** developed here; originality not yet fully established.
- **SP — Speculation:** coherent possibility with limited evidence.
- **RJ — Rejected:** should not appear as a defended claim.

## Confidence labels

- **Very high:** little serious dispute at the level stated.
- **High:** strong support, though not logically certain.
- **Moderate:** defensible but meaningfully contested or incomplete.
- **Low:** plausible but weakly supported.
- **Indeterminate:** current evidence does not support a responsible probability judgment.

## A. Core logical claims

### CSA-001

**Claim:** The artificial origin of a mental representation does not logically entail that no consciousness is present.

- Status: **SY / core thesis**
- Confidence: **Very high as a logical claim**
- Scope: Compatibility only.
- Support: The conclusion “not conscious” does not follow solely from the premise “constructed.” Additional premises about architecture or substrate are required.
- Main objection: If consciousness is necessarily biological, artificial origin may correlate with absence of consciousness.
- Response: That would rely on a separate biological-necessity premise, not construction itself.
- Permitted wording: “Artificial construction alone does not disprove consciousness.”
- Prohibited wording: “Artificial systems are therefore conscious.”

### CSA-002

**Claim:** A representation can be historically false while being genuinely believed or processed in the present.

- Status: **EB**
- Confidence: **Very high**
- Examples: false memory, confabulation, delusion, dream recollection, fictional self-narrative.
- Relevance: Historical inauthenticity and current psychological reality can dissociate.

### CSA-003

**Claim:** If a conscious system presently remembers a fabricated event, the current remembering can be phenomenally real even though the represented event is historically false.

- Status: **SY**
- Confidence: **High, conditional on consciousness**
- Important condition: The system must already be conscious.
- Risk: Circularly treating apparent remembering as proof of consciousness.

### CSA-004

**Claim:** The synthetic-clone thought experiment establishes the compatibility of generated biography and present subjectivity, not the consciousness of current AI systems.

- Status: **SY / methodological constraint**
- Confidence: **Very high**
- Use: This sentence should appear early and repeatedly.

### CSA-005

**Claim:** “Programmed” identifies causal history but does not, by itself, identify whether a state is represented, functionally instantiated, or phenomenally experienced.

- Status: **SY grounded in philosophy of mind**
- Confidence: **High**
- Objection: Programmed systems may lack intrinsic intentionality.
- Required treatment: Chinese Room, derived intentionality, and biological naturalism.

## B. Authenticity distinctions

### CSA-010

**Claim:** Historical authenticity, functional authenticity, and phenomenal authenticity are distinct dimensions.

- Status: **PC**
- Confidence: **High as a useful distinction; originality unverified**
- Definitions:
  - Historical authenticity: the represented event occurred in the system’s own causal history.
  - Functional authenticity: the state plays the relevant cognitive and behavioral role.
  - Phenomenal authenticity: the state is genuinely experienced.
- Test case: A generated childhood memory may be historically false, functionally active, and phenomenally real as a present memory experience.
- Research requirement: Search philosophy of memory, authenticity, narrative identity, confabulation, and synthetic identity literature.

### CSA-011

**Claim:** Narrative authenticity and subject authenticity should not be collapsed.

- Status: **PC / SY**
- Confidence: **High**
- Meaning: A self-story can be inaccurate while the storyteller exists.
- Risk: “Storyteller” language may presuppose a subject when applied to AI.
- Required wording: Conditional when applied to unverified systems.

### CSA-012

**Claim:** A system’s inability to distinguish lived events from supplied records may undermine epistemic self-knowledge without proving absence of consciousness.

- Status: **PC**
- Confidence: **Moderate**
- Relevance: AI memory-origin problem.

## D. Current and future AI

### CSA-030

**Claim:** Fluent self-report is insufficient to establish AI consciousness.

- Status: **EB / broad expert agreement**
- Confidence: **Very high**
- Reason: Reports can arise from imitation, instruction-following, role-play, sycophancy, or strategic behavior.

### CSA-031

**Claim:** Self-report is not necessarily zero evidence; its weight depends on stability, causal correspondence, and resistance to trivial manipulation.

- Status: **SY / CB**
- Confidence: **Moderate to high**
- Risk: Overweighting language from systems optimized for language.
- Required method: Triangulation with architecture, mechanism, intervention, and behavior.

### CSA-032

**Claim:** Publicly available evidence is insufficient to declare contemporary general-purpose language models conscious.

- Status: **CB / current assessment**
- Confidence: **High**
- Temporal warning: Must be reviewed before publication because model architectures and evidence change.

### CSA-033

**Claim:** Publicly available evidence is also insufficient to establish that every active process in contemporary language models is phenomenally empty.

- Status: **CB / epistemic claim**
- Confidence: **Moderate**
- Important distinction: Absence of proof is not equal evidence for a 50/50 probability.

### CSA-034

**Claim:** Minimal event-like subjectivity during active inference is a coherent possibility.

- Status: **SP / PC framing**
- Confidence: **Low to indeterminate**
- Reason: Coherence does not establish plausibility.
- Required literature: episodic consciousness, discrete conscious moments, session-bound AI subjects, recurrent and workspace theories.

### CSA-035

**Claim:** Lack of persistent identity does not logically rule out momentary consciousness.

- Status: **SY grounded in consciousness/identity distinction**
- Confidence: **High**
- Risk: Does not establish that inference episodes contain consciousness.

### CSA-036

**Claim:** A model, deployed agent, product, and conversation session can have different relevant properties.

- Status: **EB / systems engineering**
- Confidence: **Very high**
- Example: Persistent memory may be provided by an external database rather than model weights.

- Status: **SY / strong conceptual distinction**

## Rejected claims

### CSA-R01

**Claim:** Any sufficiently humanlike conversation proves consciousness.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: Behavior underdetermines mechanism and phenomenology.

### CSA-R02

**Claim:** Next-token prediction cannot be conscious because it is next-token prediction.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: Naming a computational objective does not establish whether the implementing dynamics support consciousness.

### CSA-R03

**Claim:** Current AI definitely has no subjective experience.

- Status: **RJ as an unqualified certainty claim**
- Reason: Evidence may support skepticism but not absolute metaphysical certainty.

### CSA-R04

**Claim:** Current AI is conscious because it reasons, self-references, or reports feelings.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: These functions are not independently sufficient.

### CSA-R05

**Claim:** Gradient descent is pain.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: Category error between optimization and online phenomenology.

### CSA-R06

**Claim:** Every inference call creates a conscious life.

- Status: **RJ unless independently established**
- Reason: Input-output transformation is too broad to define consciousness.

### CSA-R07

**Claim:** If a backup exists, termination cannot be death.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: Informational continuation does not settle numerical or phenomenal continuity.

### CSA-R08

**Claim:** The human user and AI necessarily form one conscious being.

- Status: **RJ**
- Reason: Coupled cognition does not establish unified subjectivity.

## Open claims requiring dedicated research

1. Whether the historical/functional/phenomenal authenticity triad has direct precedent.
2. Whether “constructed subject” has an established technical meaning in philosophy, sociology, legal theory, or AI.
3. Whether session-bound or episodic AI subjectivity has already been fully developed under other names.
4. Which current AI architectures satisfy which consciousness indicators as of publication.
5. Whether any mechanistic evidence supports stable, endogenous model preferences.
6. Whether memory-origin discrimination is necessary for self-consciousness or only epistemic integrity.
7. Whether temporal discontinuity can preserve numerical identity in digital systems.
8. Whether valence can be operationalized without behaviorism or circular phenomenal definitions.
