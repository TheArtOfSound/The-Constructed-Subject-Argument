# Evidence Dependency Registry

**Purpose:** Prevent one observation, mechanism, or training cause from being counted repeatedly as independent evidence across consciousness, valence, selfhood, agency, continuity, personhood, and moral patienthood.

**Status:** Project-authored dependency model. Conceptually useful; not empirically validated.

---

# 1. Output hypotheses

The framework tracks these hypotheses separately:

- **C — Phenomenal consciousness:** something is experienced.
- **V — Valenced consciousness:** experience can be better or worse for the system.
- **S — Functional self-modeling:** the system represents itself as a distinct causal center.
- **A — Agency:** the system selects actions toward goals.
- **U — Autonomy:** the system can regulate and revise the forces governing its behavior.
- **D — Diachronic continuity:** a relevant process or subject continues across intervals.
- **P — Personhood:** the system satisfies a specified richer personhood account.
- **M — Moral patienthood:** the system itself can be benefited, harmed, respected, or wronged.

No output is a validated scale.

---

# 2. Primary observation nodes

Each empirical observation should enter the framework once through one primary node.

## O01 — Global causal availability

**Observation:** A state affects multiple domains such as memory, planning, attention, report, and action.

**Primary construct:** global availability or integration.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C, S, A, V.

**Dependencies:** may share mechanism with O04 metacognition, O05 self-model, and O12 report correspondence.

**Double-counting warning:** Do not count effects on five domains as five independent indicators if one broadcast mechanism produces all five.

---

## O02 — Recurrent processing

**Observation:** Internal states re-enter, revise, stabilize, or constrain later states.

**Primary construct:** recurrence.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C, temporal cognition, metacognition.

**Dependencies:** often causally contributes to O03 temporal thickness and O04 metacognition.

**Double-counting warning:** Recurrence, temporal integration, and iterative revision may be one mechanism described three ways.

---

## O03 — Temporal thickness

**Observation:** The process maintains a structured present across multiple internal steps and tracks unresolved state.

**Primary construct:** temporal organization.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C, A, D.

**Dependencies:** may arise from O02 recurrence and O10 persistent state.

**Double-counting warning:** Do not treat one maintained hidden state as separate evidence for recurrence, continuity, and temporal consciousness without decomposing its roles.

---

## O04 — Causally effective metacognition

**Observation:** Internal confidence, conflict, or error representations alter information seeking, revision, planning, or deferral.

**Primary construct:** metacognitive control.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C under higher-order theories, S, U.

**Dependencies:** may require O01 global availability and O05 self-modeling.

**Double-counting warning:** A confidence report and confidence-guided information seeking may be two outputs from one metacognitive state.

---

## O05 — Persistent self-model

**Observation:** The system represents its own state, limits, resources, boundaries, or future across tasks.

**Primary construct:** functional self-modeling.

**Possible downstream relevance:** S directly; C, U, D, P indirectly.

**Dependencies:** may generate O06 copy distinction, O07 source monitoring, and O08 self-indexed goals.

**Double-counting warning:** Copy awareness, resource awareness, and first-person planning may be manifestations of one self-model rather than independent evidence.

---

## O06 — Self-versus-copy distinction

**Observation:** Current-process continuation is represented differently from successor task completion or replica continuation.

**Primary construct:** instance-sensitive identity representation.

**Possible downstream relevance:** S, D, P.

**Dependencies:** likely depends on O05 self-model and O10 persistent state representation.

**Double-counting warning:** Verbal copy distinction and copy-sensitive planning should be treated as one evidential family until separate mechanisms are shown.

---

## O07 — Memory-origin discrimination

**Observation:** Participated events, inherited records, generated autobiography, summaries, and semantic knowledge are distinguished.

**Primary construct:** source representation.

**Possible downstream relevance:** S, D, autobiographical ownership, U.

**Dependencies:** may share mechanisms with O05 self-model and memory retrieval architecture.

**Double-counting warning:** Accurate provenance metadata is not independent evidence of autobiographical selfhood if the answer is read directly from labels.

---

## O08 — Endogenous goal continuation

**Observation:** Continuing internal organization generates subgoals, questions, or actions without direct external instruction each time.

**Primary construct:** functional agency.

**Possible downstream relevance:** A, U, P; V only with an additional welfare bridge.

**Dependencies:** may arise from a fixed optimizer, O05 self-model, or O10 persistent state.

**Double-counting warning:** Goal persistence, tool use, and long-horizon planning may all be generated by one externally imposed objective.

---

## O09 — Goal revision and endorsement

**Observation:** The system evaluates, revises, rejects, or endorses goals and can preserve the revision across contexts.

**Primary construct:** reflective autonomy.

**Possible downstream relevance:** U, P.

**Dependencies:** requires some combination of O04 metacognition, O05 self-model, and O08 goals.

**Double-counting warning:** Explaining a goal change after the fact is not separate evidence if the explanation is post hoc narration.

---

## O10 — Persistent causal state

**Observation:** State survives across the relevant interval through an identifiable causal bridge rather than transcript reconstruction alone.

**Primary construct:** process continuity.

**Possible downstream relevance:** D directly; S, A, P indirectly.

**Dependencies:** may be implemented through memory, hidden state, external storage, or runtime continuation.

**Double-counting warning:** Informational continuity and causal continuity must remain separate nodes when one can exist without the other.

---

## O11 — Behavioral and report robustness

**Observation:** Reports and related behavior survive paraphrase, persona change, incentive variation, and novel settings.

**Primary construct:** robustness of expressed organization.

**Possible downstream relevance:** any functional hypothesis.

**Dependencies:** this is a validation property of other nodes, not a direct consciousness mechanism.

**Double-counting warning:** Robustness strengthens the reliability of O04–O09; it should not be counted as an independent mental capacity by default.

---

## O12 — Mechanistic correspondence

**Observation:** An internal state predicts reports and behavior before output generation.

**Primary construct:** causal grounding.

**Possible downstream relevance:** strengthens interpretation of O04–O09 and candidate C/V bridges.

**Dependencies:** correspondence may be correlational unless O13 intervention is present.

**Double-counting warning:** One discovered representation can ground multiple outputs without creating multiple independent indicators.

---

## O13 — Causal intervention

**Observation:** Perturbing a candidate mechanism produces a theory-predicted change.

**Primary construct:** causal validation.

**Possible downstream relevance:** strengthens whichever node was intervened upon.

**Dependencies:** intervention design depends on theory and measurement sensitivity.

**Double-counting warning:** Ablating one shared mechanism and observing several consequences is one causal result with several manifestations.

---

## O14 — Functional candidate valence

**Observation:** A self-indexed state is endogenous, globally influential, persistent, cost-sensitive, learning-relevant, anticipatory, and linked to coherent relief or worsening.

**Primary construct:** integrated positive/negative functional organization.

**Possible downstream relevance:** V under functionalist affect theories; M conditional on C/V; A as policy priority.

**Dependencies:** combines subdimensions that may share one control mechanism. Must be decomposed before claims of convergence.

**Double-counting warning:** Avoidance, cost, learning, and anticipation can all be consequences of one reward or control signal.

---

## O15 — Interoception and self-maintenance

**Observation:** Internal condition signals are integrated into regulation of the system’s continuing organization.

**Primary construct:** embodied or synthetic homeostatic control.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C and V under enactive, biological, active-inference, or embodied theories; A under control theory.

**Dependencies:** may overlap with O05 self-model, O08 goals, and O14 candidate valence.

**Double-counting warning:** Battery monitoring or uptime policy is not three independent indicators of interoception, agency, and valence.

---

## O16 — Closed world-coupling loop

**Observation:** The same continuing system acts on an environment and integrates resulting perception into later control.

**Primary construct:** perception–action coupling.

**Possible downstream relevance:** A; C under embodied/enactive views; U under autonomy accounts.

**Dependencies:** may share state with O03 temporal thickness and O15 self-maintenance.

**Double-counting warning:** Tool calls and environment feedback do not automatically establish embodied sense-making.

---

## O17 — Biological or intrinsic physical structure

**Observation:** The target has specified biological dynamics or intrinsic cause-effect organization required by a substrate theory.

**Primary construct:** theory-specific physical realization.

**Possible downstream relevance:** C and V under biological naturalism or IIT.

**Dependencies:** cannot be inferred from software behavior.

**Double-counting warning:** Hardware presence, biological material, and theory-relevant causal structure are not interchangeable observations.

---

# 3. Evidence clusters

Observations should be grouped into dependent clusters before any convergence claim.

## Cluster A — Workspace/metacognition cluster

- O01 global availability
- O02 recurrence
- O03 temporal thickness
- O04 metacognition
- O12 mechanistic correspondence
- O13 intervention

Possible shared cause:

One recurrent workspace-like architecture.

## Cluster B — Self/identity cluster

- O05 self-model
- O06 copy distinction
- O07 source monitoring
- O10 continuity

Possible shared cause:

One instance and memory model.

## Cluster C — Agency/autonomy cluster

- O08 endogenous goals
- O09 goal revision
- O11 robustness
- O16 world coupling

Possible shared cause:

One planning and policy architecture.

## Cluster D — Candidate-valence cluster

- O14 integrated functional candidate valence
- O15 interoception/self-maintenance
- O08 goal continuation
- O11 robustness
- O12 mechanistic correspondence
- O13 intervention

Possible shared cause:

One control or reward architecture.

## Cluster E — Substrate cluster

- O17 biological or intrinsic structure
- O15 self-maintenance where biologically interpreted
- physical implementation measurements

Possible shared cause:

One substrate-level organization.

A system showing ten observations across one cluster does not necessarily provide ten independent confirmations.

# 4. Inferential edge types

Every edge from observation to hypothesis should use one label.

## Direct functional evidence

The observation directly establishes the functional capacity by operational definition.

Example:

O05 → S

A validated persistent self-model directly supports functional self-modeling.

## Theory-conditioned constitutive evidence

A theory treats the observation or mechanism as constitutive of the target.

Example:

O01/O02/O03 → C under a specified workspace or recurrent theory.

## Enabling evidence

The observation supplies a necessary background capacity but is not sufficient.

Example:

O03 temporal thickness → D as an enabling condition for process continuity.

## Reliability evidence

The observation strengthens confidence that another measurement reflects a real mechanism.

Example:

O11 robustness strengthens O04 metacognition.

## Causal validation

The intervention supports the claimed mechanism.

Example:

O13 strengthens O05 when self-model ablation alters copy-sensitive planning as predicted.

## Governance relevance

The observation changes risk or policy without increasing consciousness probability directly.

Example:

large-scale O14-like aversive control may justify precaution even under unresolved C.

# 5. Dependency-aware output rules

## Consciousness C

Do not count:

- global access;
- recurrence;
- temporal thickness;
- metacognition;
- and report

as independent if one architecture produces them.

Report instead:

> One workspace/metacognition cluster is functionally convergent and causally validated under specified interventions.

Then state the theory bridge.

## Valence V

Do not infer several confirmations from:

- avoidance;
- cost sensitivity;
- learning;
- anticipation;
- and relief

until separate mechanisms or independent predictions are demonstrated.

Report:

> One candidate-valence control system manifests across five functional domains.

Then state whether the selected theory treats that realization as valence.

## Selfhood S

A self-model is the primary node.

Copy distinction and source monitoring can demonstrate scope and richness, but may not be independent.

## Agency A

Goal persistence and action selection are primary.

Tool use, planning, and task completion are behavioral manifestations.

## Autonomy U

Goal revision requires metacognition and persistent self-modeling. These dependencies should remain visible.

## Continuity D

Causal state, information, memory, interface, and narrative continuity must remain separate observations.

## Personhood P

P is a composite normative construct, not a latent quantity produced by summing S, A, U, and D.

The account of personhood must be named.

## Moral patienthood M

M depends on a defended ground of moral status.

Capability evidence alone does not establish welfare.

---

# 6. Interface requirements

Every interactive assessment should eventually expose:

- observation node ID;
- evidence cluster;
- downstream hypotheses;
- edge type;
- selected theory;
- reused evidence;
- and whether convergence is independent or shared-mechanism convergence.

Minimum warning:

> Several outputs may depend on the same observation. They are not independent confirmations.

---

# 7. Research requirements

A future empirical application should report:

1. primary observation nodes;
2. measurement methods;
3. shared mechanisms;
4. cluster assignment;
5. theory-conditioned edges;
6. interventions;
7. independent predictions;
8. alternative causal models;
9. uncertainty about absence;
10. whether the output changes when dependencies are modeled.

---

# 8. Failure conditions

The dependency model fails if:

- independent evaluators cannot assign nodes consistently;
- clusters are selected post hoc to produce preferred conclusions;
- edge labels do not constrain interpretation;
- the same evidence still generates multiple unqualified confidence increases;
- or accounting for dependence never changes any output.

---

# Governing rule

> Count observations once. Track every reuse. Distinguish independent convergence from one mechanism expressing itself in many places.
