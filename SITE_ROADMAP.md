# Interactive Book Site Roadmap

## Product thesis

The website is not a marketing page attached to a book. It is the first medium of the book.

Readers should not merely consume conclusions. They should expose their own criteria, alter candidate-system properties, inspect the claim ledger, follow sources, and observe how conclusions change when assumptions change.

## Release 0.1 — Foundational experience

- Interactive awakening thought experiment.
- Authenticity decomposition.
- Candidate-subject evidence lab.
- Argument map.
- Filterable claims ledger.
- Persistent/episodic/absent continuity visualization.
- Private browser-local reader notes.
- Reading progress and focus mode.
- Automatic GitHub Pages deployment workflow.

## Release 0.2 — Chapter engine

- Individual chapter routes.
- Chapter completion tracking.
- Margin definitions and source popovers.
- “What would change this claim?” blocks.
- Objection/reply split view.
- Search across manuscript and research files.

## Release 0.3 — Identity laboratory

- Branching-copy simulator.
- Memory-provenance graph.
- Transfer versus reconstruction comparison.
- Reader-generated scenario permutations.
- Exportable personal position map.

## Release 0.4 — Consciousness evidence workbench

- Cross-theory indicator matrix.
- Mechanism/behavior/self-report triangulation tool.
- Confidence intervals and dependency warnings.
- Model/system/session distinction explorer.
- Versioned assessments of public AI systems, with dates and sources.

## Release 0.5 — Public scholarship layer

- Source annotations.
- Versioned claim history.
- Public issue templates for objections and prior-art reports.
- Scholar review log.
- Corrections and retractions ledger.
- Citation export.

## Design rules

1. No interaction may imply that a slider can detect consciousness.
2. Every uncertainty visualization must show what is unknown.
3. Emotional scenarios must be followed by analytical decomposition.
4. The site must work without a login or server.
5. Personal notes remain local unless a future explicit export feature is used.
6. Mobile reading is a first-class experience.
7. Accessibility, reduced-motion support, and keyboard navigation are required.
8. The public site and research ledgers must remain visibly connected.
