# Contributing

This project welcomes rigorous criticism, prior-art reports, source corrections, falsification attempts, accessibility fixes, and improvements to the interactive book.

It does not accept contributions whose primary function is to declare that AI is obviously conscious or obviously incapable of consciousness.

## Useful contributions

### Prior art

Identify a source that anticipates a proposed contribution.

Include:

- full citation and stable link;
- the exact claim or distinction it anticipates;
- a short explanation of the overlap;
- the material difference, if any;
- whether the originality ledger should be downgraded.

### Strong objections

Present the strongest version of an objection.

Include:

- the target claim ID from `research/CLAIMS_LEDGER.md`;
- the objection in a form its strongest defender would accept;
- the premise or inference it attacks;
- evidence or sources;
- what revision would resolve the problem.

### Source corrections

Identify:

- the exact sentence or citation;
- what the cited source actually supports;
- the corrected language;
- a primary source where possible.

### Interactive-book improvements

Changes should improve at least one of:

- conceptual understanding;
- reader participation;
- accessibility;
- mobile reading;
- epistemic transparency;
- research traceability;
- performance or reliability.

Interactions must not imply that a slider, score, or quiz can detect consciousness.

## Required standards

1. Separate consciousness, sentience, agency, self-consciousness, and personhood.
2. Separate the base model, session, product, agent scaffold, memory system, and user-model system.
3. Do not infer experience from humanlike language alone.
4. Do not infer absence of experience from artificial origin alone.
5. Label possibility, plausibility, probability, and certainty distinctly.
6. Prefer primary sources for scientific and technical claims.
7. Quote minimally and paraphrase accurately.
8. Disclose material use of generative AI in research or drafting.
9. State what evidence would change your conclusion.
10. Treat novelty as a claim requiring evidence.

## Pull request structure

A pull request should include:

- **Problem:** What is wrong or missing?
- **Change:** What did you alter?
- **Claim impact:** Which claim IDs or originality entries change?
- **Evidence:** Which sources support the change?
- **Counterargument:** What is the strongest objection to your revision?
- **Site impact:** Does the public interactive experience change?
- **Disclosure:** Was generative AI materially used?

## Editorial authority

Bryan Leonard retains final editorial and publication responsibility.

Submissions may be correct but declined if they duplicate planned work, weaken conceptual clarity, introduce legal risk, or cannot be integrated without distorting the book’s structure.

No contribution is accepted merely because it supports the book’s current thesis.
